## Job 7

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Is there not an appointed time to man upon earth? are not his days also like the days of an hireling?</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> As a servant earnestly desireth the shadow, and as an hireling looketh for the reward of his work:</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> So am I made to possess months of vanity, and wearisome nights are appointed to me.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> When I lie down, I say, When shall I arise, and the night be gone? and I am full of tossings to and fro unto the dawning of the day.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> My flesh is clothed with worms and clods of dust; my skin is broken, and become loathsome.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> My days are swifter than a weaver's shuttle, and are spent without hope.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> O remember that my life is wind: mine eye shall no more see good.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> The eye of him that hath seen me shall see me no more: thine eyes are upon me, and I am not.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> As the cloud is consumed and vanisheth away: so he that goeth down to the grave shall come up no more.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> He shall return no more to his house, neither shall his place know him any more.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Therefore I will not refrain my mouth; I will speak in the anguish of my spirit; I will complain in the bitterness of my soul.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> Am I a sea, or a whale, that thou settest a watch over me?</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> When I say, My bed shall comfort me, my couch shall ease my complaint;</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> Then thou scarest me with dreams, and terrifiest me through visions:</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> So that my soul chooseth strangling, and death rather than my life.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> I loathe it; I would not live alway: let me alone; for my days are vanity.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> What is man, that thou shouldest magnify him? and that thou shouldest set thine heart upon him?</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> And that thou shouldest visit him every morning, and try him every moment?</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> How long wilt thou not depart from me, nor let me alone till I swallow down my spittle?</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> I have sinned; what shall I do unto thee, O thou preserver of men? why hast thou set me as a mark against thee, so that I am a burden to myself?</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> And why dost thou not pardon my transgression, and take away mine iniquity? for now shall I sleep in the dust; and thou shalt seek me in the morning, but I shall not be.</p>
