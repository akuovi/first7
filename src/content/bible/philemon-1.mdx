## Philemon 1

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Paul, a prisoner of Jesus Christ, and Timothy our brother, unto Philemon our dearly beloved, and fellowlabourer, 2 And to our beloved Apphia, and Archippus our fellowsoldier, and to the church in thy house:</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> Grace to you, and peace, from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> I thank my God, making mention of thee always in my prayers,</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Hearing of thy love and faith, which thou hast toward the Lord Jesus, and toward all saints;</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> That the communication of thy faith may become effectual by the acknowledging of every good thing which is in you in Christ Jesus.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> For we have great joy and consolation in thy love, because the bowels of the saints are refreshed by thee, brother.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> Wherefore, though I might be much bold in Christ to enjoin thee that which is convenient,</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Yet for love's sake I rather beseech thee, being such an one as Paul the aged, and now also a prisoner of Jesus Christ.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> I beseech thee for my son Onesimus, whom I have begotten in my bonds:</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Which in time past was to thee unprofitable, but now profitable to thee and to me:</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Whom I have sent again: thou therefore receive him, that is, mine own bowels:</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> Whom I would have retained with me, that in thy stead he might have ministered unto me in the bonds of the gospel:</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> But without thy mind would I do nothing; that thy benefit should not be as it were of necessity, but willingly.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> For perhaps he therefore departed for a season, that thou shouldest receive him for ever;</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> Not now as a servant, but above a servant, a brother beloved, specially to me, but how much more unto thee, both in the flesh, and in the Lord?</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> If thou count me therefore a partner, receive him as myself.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> If he hath wronged thee, or oweth thee ought, put that on mine account;</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> I Paul have written it with mine own hand, I will repay it: albeit I do not say to thee how thou owest unto me even thine own self besides.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> Yea, brother, let me have joy of thee in the Lord: refresh my bowels in the Lord.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> Having confidence in thy obedience I wrote unto thee, knowing that thou wilt also do more than I say.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> But withal prepare me also a lodging: for I trust that through your prayers I shall be given unto you.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> There salute thee Epaphras, my fellowprisoner in Christ Jesus;</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> Marcus, Aristarchus, Demas, Lucas, my fellowlabourers.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with your spirit. Amen.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> Written from Rome to Philemon, by Onesimus, a servant.</p>
