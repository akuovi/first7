## Genesis 50

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> And Joseph fell upon his father's face, and wept upon him, and kissed him.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> And Joseph commanded his servants the physicians to embalm his father: and the physicians embalmed Israel.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> And forty days were fulfilled for him; for so are fulfilled the days of those which are embalmed: and the Egyptians mourned for him threescore and ten days.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> And when the days of his mourning were past, Joseph spake unto the house of Pharaoh, saying, If now I have found grace in your eyes, speak, I pray you, in the ears of Pharaoh, saying,</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> My father made me swear, saying, Lo, I die: in my grave which I have digged for me in the land of Canaan, there shalt thou bury me. Now therefore let me go up, I pray thee, and bury my father, and I will come again.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> And Pharaoh said, Go up, and bury thy father, according as he made thee swear.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> And Joseph went up to bury his father: and with him went up all the servants of Pharaoh, the elders of his house, and all the elders of the land of Egypt,</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> And all the house of Joseph, and his brethren, and his father's house: only their little ones, and their flocks, and their herds, they left in the land of Goshen.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> And there went up with him both chariots and horsemen: and it was a very great company.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> And they came to the threshingfloor of Atad, which is beyond Jordan, and there they mourned with a great and very sore lamentation: and he made a mourning for his father seven days.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> And when the inhabitants of the land, the Canaanites, saw the mourning in the floor of Atad, they said, This is a grievous mourning to the Egyptians: wherefore the name of it was called Abel-mizraim, which is beyond Jordan.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> And his sons did unto him according as he commanded them:</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> For his sons carried him into the land of Canaan, and buried him in the cave of the field of Machpelah, which Abraham bought with the field for a possession of a buryingplace of Ephron the Hittite, before Mamre.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> And Joseph returned into Egypt, he, and his brethren, and all that went up with him to bury his father, after he had buried his father.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> And when Joseph's brethren saw that their father was dead, they said, Joseph will peradventure hate us, and will certainly requite us all the evil which we did unto him.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> And they sent a messenger unto Joseph, saying, Thy father did command before he died, saying,</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> So shall ye say unto Joseph, Forgive, I pray thee now, the trespass of thy brethren, and their sin; for they did unto thee evil: and now, we pray thee, forgive the trespass of the servants of the God of thy father. And Joseph wept when they spake unto him.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> And his brethren also went and fell down before his face; and they said, Behold, we be thy servants.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> And Joseph said unto them, Fear not: for am I in the place of God?</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> But as for you, ye thought evil against me; but God meant it unto good, to bring to pass, as it is this day, to save much people alive.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> Now therefore fear ye not: I will nourish you, and your little ones. And he comforted them, and spake kindly unto them.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> And Joseph dwelt in Egypt, he, and his father's house: and Joseph lived an hundred and ten years.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> And Joseph saw Ephraim's children of the third generation: the children also of Machir the son of Manasseh were brought up upon Joseph's knees.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> And Joseph said unto his brethren, I die: and God will surely visit you, and bring you out of this land unto the land which he sware to Abraham, to Isaac, and to Jacob.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> And Joseph took an oath of the children of Israel, saying, God will surely visit you, and ye shall carry up my bones from hence.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> So Joseph died, being an hundred and ten years old: and they embalmed him, and he was put in a coffin in Egypt.</p>
