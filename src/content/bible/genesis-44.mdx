## Genesis 44

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> And he commanded the steward of his house, saying, Fill the men's sacks with food, as much as they can carry, and put every man's money in his sack's mouth.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> And put my cup, the silver cup, in the sack's mouth of the youngest, and his corn money. And he did according to the word that Joseph had spoken.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> As soon as the morning was light, the men were sent away, they and their asses.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> And when they were gone out of the city, and not yet far off, Joseph said unto his steward, Up, follow after the men; and when thou dost overtake them, say unto them, Wherefore have ye rewarded evil for good?</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Is not this it in which my lord drinketh, and whereby indeed he divineth? ye have done evil in so doing.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> And he overtook them, and he spake unto them these same words.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> And they said unto him, Wherefore saith my lord these words? God forbid that thy servants should do according to this thing:</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Behold, the money, which we found in our sacks' mouths, we brought again unto thee out of the land of Canaan: how then should we steal out of thy lord's house silver or gold?</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> With whomsoever of thy servants it be found, both let him die, and we also will be my lord's bondmen.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> And he said, Now also let it be according unto your words: he with whom it is found shall be my servant; and ye shall be blameless.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Then they speedily took down every man his sack to the ground, and opened every man his sack.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> And he searched, and began at the eldest, and left at the youngest: and the cup was found in Benjamin's sack.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Then they rent their clothes, and laded every man his ass, and returned to the city.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> And Judah and his brethren came to Joseph's house; for he was yet there: and they fell before him on the ground.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> And Joseph said unto them, What deed is this that ye have done? wot ye not that such a man as I can certainly divine?</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> And Judah said, What shall we say unto my lord? what shall we speak? or how shall we clear ourselves? God hath found out the iniquity of thy servants: behold, we are my lord's servants, both we, and he also with whom the cup is found.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> And he said, God forbid that I should do so: but the man in whose hand the cup is found, he shall be my servant; and as for you, get you up in peace unto your father.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> Then Judah came near unto him, and said, Oh my lord, let thy servant, I pray thee, speak a word in my lord's ears, and let not thine anger burn against thy servant: for thou art even as Pharaoh.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> My lord asked his servants, saying, Have ye a father, or a brother?</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> And we said unto my lord, We have a father, an old man, and a child of his old age, a little one; and his brother is dead, and he alone is left of his mother, and his father loveth him.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> And thou saidst unto thy servants, Bring him down unto me, that I may set mine eyes upon him.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> And we said unto my lord, The lad cannot leave his father: for if he should leave his father, his father would die.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> And thou saidst unto thy servants, Except your youngest brother come down with you, ye shall see my face no more.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> And it came to pass when we came up unto thy servant my father, we told him the words of my lord.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> And our father said, Go again, and buy us a little food.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> And we said, We cannot go down: if our youngest brother be with us, then will we go down: for we may not see the man's face, except our youngest brother be with us.</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> And thy servant my father said unto us, Ye know that my wife bare me two sons:</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> And the one went out from me, and I said, Surely he is torn in pieces; and I saw him not since:</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> And if ye take this also from me, and mischief befall him, ye shall bring down my gray hairs with sorrow to the grave.</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> Now therefore when I come to thy servant my father, and the lad be not with us; seeing that his life is bound up in the lad's life;</p>

<p id="v31" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">31</span> It shall come to pass, when he seeth that the lad is not with us, that he will die: and thy servants shall bring down the gray hairs of thy servant our father with sorrow to the grave.</p>

<p id="v32" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">32</span> For thy servant became surety for the lad unto my father, saying, If I bring him not unto thee, then I shall bear the blame to my father for ever.</p>

<p id="v33" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">33</span> Now therefore, I pray thee, let thy servant abide instead of the lad a bondman to my lord; and let the lad go up with his brethren.</p>

<p id="v34" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">34</span> For how shall I go up to my father, and the lad be not with me? lest peradventure I see the evil that shall come on my father.</p>
