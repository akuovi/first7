## Numbers 23

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> And Balaam said unto Balak, Build me here seven altars, and prepare me here seven oxen and seven rams.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> And Balak did as Balaam had spoken; and Balak and Balaam offered on every altar a bullock and a ram.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> And Balaam said unto Balak, Stand by thy burnt offering, and I will go: peradventure the LORD will come to meet me: and whatsoever he sheweth me I will tell thee. And he went to an high place.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> And God met Balaam: and he said unto him, I have prepared seven altars, and I have offered upon every altar a bullock and a ram.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> And the LORD put a word in Balaam's mouth, and said, Return unto Balak, and thus thou shalt speak.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> And he returned unto him, and, lo, he stood by his burnt sacrifice, he, and all the princes of Moab.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> And he took up his parable, and said, Balak the king of Moab hath brought me from Aram, out of the mountains of the east, saying, Come, curse me Jacob, and come, defy Israel.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> How shall I curse, whom God hath not cursed? or how shall I defy, whom the LORD hath not defied?</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> For from the top of the rocks I see him, and from the hills I behold him: lo, the people shall dwell alone, and shall not be reckoned among the nations.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Who can count the dust of Jacob, and the number of the fourth part of Israel? Let me die the death of the righteous, and let my last end be like his!</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> And Balak said unto Balaam, What hast thou done unto me? I took thee to curse mine enemies, and, behold, thou hast blessed them altogether.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> And he answered and said, Must I not take heed to speak that which the LORD hath put in my mouth?</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> And Balak said unto him, Come, I pray thee, with me unto another place, from whence thou mayest see them: thou shalt see but the utmost part of them, and shalt not see them all: and curse me them from thence.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> And he brought him into the field of Zophim, to the top of Pisgah, and built seven altars, and offered a bullock and a ram on every altar.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> And he said unto Balak, Stand here by thy burnt offering, while I meet the LORD yonder.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> And the LORD met Balaam, and put a word in his mouth, and said, Go again unto Balak, and say thus.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> And when he came to him, behold, he stood by his burnt offering, and the princes of Moab with him. And Balak said unto him, What hath the LORD spoken?</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> And he took up his parable, and said, Rise up, Balak, and hear; hearken unto me, thou son of Zippor:</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> God is not a man, that he should lie; neither the son of man, that he should repent: hath he said, and shall he not do it? or hath he spoken, and shall he not make it good?</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> Behold, I have received commandment to bless: and he hath blessed; and I cannot reverse it.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> He hath not beheld iniquity in Jacob, neither hath he seen perverseness in Israel: the LORD his God is with him, and the shout of a king is among them.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> God brought them out of Egypt; he hath as it were the strength of an unicorn.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> Surely there is no enchantment against Jacob, neither is there any divination against Israel: according to this time it shall be said of Jacob and of Israel, What hath God wrought!</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> Behold, the people shall rise up as a great lion, and lift up himself as a young lion: he shall not lie down until he eat of the prey, and drink the blood of the slain.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> And Balak said unto Balaam, Neither curse them at all, nor bless them at all.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> But Balaam answered and said unto Balak, Told not I thee, saying, All that the LORD speaketh, that I must do?</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> And Balak said unto Balaam, Come, I pray thee, I will bring thee unto another place; peradventure it will please God that thou mayest curse me them from thence.</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> And Balak brought Balaam unto the top of Peor, that looketh toward Jeshimon.</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> And Balaam said unto Balak, Build me here seven altars, and prepare me here seven bullocks and seven rams.</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> And Balak did as Balaam had said, and offered a bullock and a ram on every altar.</p>
