## Ecclesiastes 1

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> The words of the Preacher, the son of David, king in Jerusalem.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> Vanity of vanities, saith the Preacher, vanity of vanities; all is vanity.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> What profit hath a man of all his labour which he taketh under the sun?</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> One generation passeth away, and another generation cometh: but the earth abideth for ever.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> The sun also ariseth, and the sun goeth down, and hasteth to his place where he arose.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> The wind goeth toward the south, and turneth about unto the north; it whirleth about continually, and the wind returneth again according to his circuits.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> All the rivers run into the sea; yet the sea is not full; unto the place from whence the rivers come, thither they return again.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> All things are full of labour; man cannot utter it: the eye is not satisfied with seeing, nor the ear filled with hearing.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> The thing that hath been, it is that which shall be; and that which is done is that which shall be done: and there is no new thing under the sun.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Is there any thing whereof it may be said, See, this is new? it hath been already of old time, which was before us.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> There is no remembrance of former things; neither shall there be any remembrance of things that are to come with those that shall come after.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> I the Preacher was king over Israel in Jerusalem.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> And I gave my heart to seek and search out by wisdom concerning all things that are done under heaven: this sore travail hath God given to the sons of man to be exercised therewith.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> I have seen all the works that are done under the sun; and, behold, all is vanity and vexation of spirit.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> That which is crooked cannot be made straight: and that which is wanting cannot be numbered.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> I communed with mine own heart, saying, Lo, I am come to great estate, and have gotten more wisdom than all they that have been before me in Jerusalem: yea, my heart had great experience of wisdom and knowledge.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> And I gave my heart to know wisdom, and to know madness and folly: I perceived that this also is vexation of spirit.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> For in much wisdom is much grief: and he that increaseth knowledge increaseth sorrow.</p>
