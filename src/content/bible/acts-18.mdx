## Acts 18

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> After these things Paul departed from Athens, and came to Corinth;</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> And found a certain Jew named Aquila, born in Pontus, lately come from Italy, with his wife Priscilla; (because that Claudius had commanded all Jews to depart from Rome:) and came unto them.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> And because he was of the same craft, he abode with them, and wrought: for by their occupation they were tentmakers.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> And he reasoned in the synagogue every sabbath, and persuaded the Jews and the Greeks.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> And when Silas and Timotheus were come from Macedonia, Paul was pressed in the spirit, and testified to the Jews that Jesus was Christ.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> And when they opposed themselves, and blasphemed, he shook his raiment, and said unto them, Your blood be upon your own heads; I am clean: from henceforth I will go unto the Gentiles.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> And he departed thence, and entered into a certain man's house, named Justus, one that worshipped God, whose house joined hard to the synagogue.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> And Crispus, the chief ruler of the synagogue, believed on the Lord with all his house; and many of the Corinthians hearing believed, and were baptized.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Then spake the Lord to Paul in the night by a vision, Be not afraid, but speak, and hold not thy peace:</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> For I am with thee, and no man shall set on thee to hurt thee: for I have much people in this city.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> And he continued there a year and six months, teaching the word of God among them.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> And when Gallio was the deputy of Achaia, the Jews made insurrection with one accord against Paul, and brought him to the judgment seat,</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Saying, This fellow persuadeth men to worship God contrary to the law.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> And when Paul was now about to open his mouth, Gallio said unto the Jews, If it were a matter of wrong or wicked lewdness, O ye Jews, reason would that I should bear with you:</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> But if it be a question of words and names, and of your law, look ye to it; for I will be no judge of such matters.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> And he drave them from the judgment seat.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> Then all the Greeks took Sosthenes, the chief ruler of the synagogue, and beat him before the judgment seat. And Gallio cared for none of those things.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> And Paul after this tarried there yet a good while, and then took his leave of the brethren, and sailed thence into Syria, and with him Priscilla and Aquila; having shorn his head in Cenchrea: for he had a vow.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> And he came to Ephesus, and left them there: but he himself entered into the synagogue, and reasoned with the Jews.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> When they desired him to tarry longer time with them, he consented not;</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> But bade them farewell, saying, I must by all means keep this feast that cometh in Jerusalem: but I will return again unto you, if God will. And he sailed from Ephesus.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> And when he had landed at Caesarea, and gone up, and saluted the church, he went down to Antioch.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> And after he had spent some time there, he departed, and went over all the country of Galatia and Phrygia in order, strengthening all the disciples.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> And a certain Jew named Apollos, born at Alexandria, an eloquent man, and mighty in the scriptures, came to Ephesus.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> This man was instructed in the way of the Lord; and being fervent in the spirit, he spake and taught diligently the things of the Lord, knowing only the baptism of John.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> And he began to speak boldly in the synagogue: whom when Aquila and Priscilla had heard, they took him unto them, and expounded unto him the way of God more perfectly.</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> And when he was disposed to pass into Achaia, the brethren wrote, exhorting the disciples to receive him: who, when he was come, helped them much which had believed through grace:</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> For he mightily convinced the Jews, and that publickly, shewing by the scriptures that Jesus was Christ.</p>
