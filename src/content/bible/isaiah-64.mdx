## Isaiah 64

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Oh that thou wouldest rend the heavens, that thou wouldest come down, that the mountains might flow down at thy presence,</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> As when the melting fire burneth, the fire causeth the waters to boil, to make thy name known to thine adversaries, that the nations may tremble at thy presence!</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> When thou didst terrible things which we looked not for, thou camest down, the mountains flowed down at thy presence.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> For since the beginning of the world men have not heard, nor perceived by the ear, neither hath the eye seen, O God, beside thee, what he hath prepared for him that waiteth for him.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Thou meetest him that rejoiceth and worketh righteousness, those that remember thee in thy ways: behold, thou art wroth; for we have sinned: in those is continuance, and we shall be saved.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> But we are all as an unclean thing, and all our righteousnesses are as filthy rags; and we all do fade as a leaf; and our iniquities, like the wind, have taken us away.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> And there is none that calleth upon thy name, that stirreth up himself to take hold of thee: for thou hast hid thy face from us, and hast consumed us, because of our iniquities.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> But now, O LORD, thou art our father; we are the clay, and thou our potter; and we all are the work of thy hand.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Be not wroth very sore, O LORD, neither remember iniquity for ever: behold, see, we beseech thee, we are all thy people.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Thy holy cities are a wilderness, Zion is a wilderness, Jerusalem a desolation.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Our holy and our beautiful house, where our fathers praised thee, is burned up with fire: and all our pleasant things are laid waste.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> Wilt thou refrain thyself for these things, O LORD? wilt thou hold thy peace, and afflict us very sore?</p>
