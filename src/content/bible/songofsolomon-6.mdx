## SongofSolomon 6

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Whither is thy beloved gone, O thou fairest among women? whither is thy beloved turned aside? that we may seek him with thee.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> My beloved is gone down into his garden, to the beds of spices, to feed in the gardens, and to gather lilies.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> I am my beloved's, and my beloved is mine: he feedeth among the lilies.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Thou art beautiful, O my love, as Tirzah, comely as Jerusalem, terrible as an army with banners.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Turn away thine eyes from me, for they have overcome me: thy hair is as a flock of goats that appear from Gilead.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> Thy teeth are as a flock of sheep which go up from the washing, whereof every one beareth twins, and there is not one barren among them.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> As a piece of a pomegranate are thy temples within thy locks.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> There are threescore queens, and fourscore concubines, and virgins without number.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> My dove, my undefiled is but one; she is the only one of her mother, she is the choice one of her that bare her. The daughters saw her, and blessed her; yea, the queens and the concubines, and they praised her.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Who is she that looketh forth as the morning, fair as the moon, clear as the sun, and terrible as an army with banners?</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> I went down into the garden of nuts to see the fruits of the valley, and to see whether the vine flourished, and the pomegranates budded.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> Or ever I was aware, my soul made me like the chariots of Amminadib.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Return, return, O Shulamite; return, return, that we may look upon thee. What will ye see in the Shulamite? As it were the company of two armies.</p>
