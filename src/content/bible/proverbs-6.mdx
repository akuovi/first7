## Proverbs 6

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> My son, if thou be surety for thy friend, if thou hast stricken thy hand with a stranger,</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> Thou art snared with the words of thy mouth, thou art taken with the words of thy mouth.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> Do this now, my son, and deliver thyself, when thou art come into the hand of thy friend; go, humble thyself, and make sure thy friend.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Give not sleep to thine eyes, nor slumber to thine eyelids.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Deliver thyself as a roe from the hand of the hunter, and as a bird from the hand of the fowler.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> Go to the ant, thou sluggard; consider her ways, and be wise:</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> Which having no guide, overseer, or ruler,</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Provideth her meat in the summer, and gathereth her food in the harvest.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> How long wilt thou sleep, O sluggard? when wilt thou arise out of thy sleep?</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Yet a little sleep, a little slumber, a little folding of the hands to sleep:</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> So shall thy poverty come as one that travelleth, and thy want as an armed man.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> A naughty person, a wicked man, walketh with a froward mouth.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> He winketh with his eyes, he speaketh with his feet, he teacheth with his fingers;</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> Frowardness is in his heart, he deviseth mischief continually; he soweth discord.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> Therefore shall his calamity come suddenly; suddenly shall he be broken without remedy.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> These six things doth the LORD hate: yea, seven are an abomination unto him:</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> A proud look, a lying tongue, and hands that shed innocent blood,</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> An heart that deviseth wicked imaginations, feet that be swift in running to mischief,</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> A false witness that speaketh lies, and he that soweth discord among brethren.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> My son, keep thy father's commandment, and forsake not the law of thy mother:</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> Bind them continually upon thine heart, and tie them about thy neck.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> When thou goest, it shall lead thee; when thou sleepest, it shall keep thee; and when thou awakest, it shall talk with thee.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> For the commandment is a lamp; and the law is light; and reproofs of instruction are the way of life:</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> To keep thee from the evil woman, from the flattery of the tongue of a strange woman.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> Lust not after her beauty in thine heart; neither let her take thee with her eyelids.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> For by means of a whorish woman a man is brought to a piece of bread: and the adulteress will hunt for the precious life.</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> Can a man take fire in his bosom, and his clothes not be burned?</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> Can one go upon hot coals, and his feet not be burned?</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> So he that goeth in to his neighbour's wife; whosoever toucheth her shall not be innocent.</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> Men do not despise a thief, if he steal to satisfy his soul when he is hungry;</p>

<p id="v31" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">31</span> But if he be found, he shall restore sevenfold; he shall give all the substance of his house.</p>

<p id="v32" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">32</span> But whoso committeth adultery with a woman lacketh understanding: he that doeth it destroyeth his own soul.</p>

<p id="v33" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">33</span> A wound and dishonour shall he get; and his reproach shall not be wiped away.</p>

<p id="v34" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">34</span> For jealousy is the rage of a man: therefore he will not spare in the day of vengeance.</p>

<p id="v35" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">35</span> He will not regard any ransom; neither will he rest content, though thou givest many gifts.</p>
