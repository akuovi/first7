## Habakkuk 1

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> The burden which Habakkuk the prophet did see.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> O LORD, how long shall I cry, and thou wilt not hear! even cry out unto thee of violence, and thou wilt not save!</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> Why dost thou shew me iniquity, and cause me to behold grievance? for spoiling and violence are before me: and there are that raise up strife and contention.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Therefore the law is slacked, and judgment doth never go forth: for the wicked doth compass about the righteous; therefore wrong judgment proceedeth.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Behold ye among the heathen, and regard, and wonder marvellously: for I will work a work in your days, which ye will not believe, though it be told you.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> For, lo, I raise up the Chaldeans, that bitter and hasty nation, which shall march through the breadth of the land, to possess the dwellingplaces that are not their's.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> They are terrible and dreadful: their judgment and their dignity shall proceed of themselves.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Their horses also are swifter than the leopards, and are more fierce than the evening wolves: and their horsemen shall spread themselves, and their horsemen shall come from far; they shall fly as the eagle that hasteth to eat.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> They shall come all for violence: their faces shall sup up as the east wind, and they shall gather the captivity as the sand.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> And they shall scoff at the kings, and the princes shall be a scorn unto them: they shall deride every strong hold; for they shall heap dust, and take it.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Then shall his mind change, and he shall pass over, and offend, imputing this his power unto his god.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> Art thou not from everlasting, O LORD my God, mine Holy One? we shall not die. O LORD, thou hast ordained them for judgment; and, O mighty God, thou hast established them for correction.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Thou art of purer eyes than to behold evil, and canst not look on iniquity: wherefore lookest thou upon them that deal treacherously, and holdest thy tongue when the wicked devoureth the man that is more righteous than he?</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> And makest men as the fishes of the sea, as the creeping things, that have no ruler over them?</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> They take up all of them with the angle, they catch them in their net, and gather them in their drag: therefore they rejoice and are glad.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> Therefore they sacrifice unto their net, and burn incense unto their drag; because by them their portion is fat, and their meat plenteous.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> Shall they therefore empty their net, and not spare continually to slay the nations?</p>
