## Job 40

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Moreover the LORD answered Job, and said,</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> Shall he that contendeth with the Almighty instruct him? he that reproveth God, let him answer it.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> Then Job answered the LORD, and said,</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Behold, I am vile; what shall I answer thee? I will lay mine hand upon my mouth.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Once have I spoken; but I will not answer: yea, twice; but I will proceed no further.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> Then answered the LORD unto Job out of the whirlwind, and said,</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> Gird up thy loins now like a man: I will demand of thee, and declare thou unto me.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Wilt thou also disannul my judgment? wilt thou condemn me, that thou mayest be righteous?</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Hast thou an arm like God? or canst thou thunder with a voice like him?</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Deck thyself now with majesty and excellency; and array thyself with glory and beauty.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Cast abroad the rage of thy wrath: and behold every one that is proud, and abase him.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> Look on every one that is proud, and bring him low; and tread down the wicked in their place.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Hide them in the dust together; and bind their faces in secret.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> Then will I also confess unto thee that thine own right hand can save thee.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> Behold now behemoth, which I made with thee; he eateth grass as an ox.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> Lo now, his strength is in his loins, and his force is in the navel of his belly.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> He moveth his tail like a cedar: the sinews of his stones are wrapped together.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> His bones are as strong pieces of brass; his bones are like bars of iron.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> He is the chief of the ways of God: he that made him can make his sword to approach unto him.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> Surely the mountains bring him forth food, where all the beasts of the field play.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> He lieth under the shady trees, in the covert of the reed, and fens.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> The shady trees cover him with their shadow; the willows of the brook compass him about.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> Behold, he drinketh up a river, and hasteth not: he trusteth that he can draw up Jordan into his mouth.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> He taketh it with his eyes: his nose pierceth through snares.</p>
