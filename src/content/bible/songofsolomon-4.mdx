## SongofSolomon 4

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Behold, thou art fair, my love; behold, thou art fair; thou hast doves' eyes within thy locks: thy hair is as a flock of goats, that appear from mount Gilead.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> Thy teeth are like a flock of sheep that are even shorn, which came up from the washing; whereof every one bear twins, and none is barren among them.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> Thy lips are like a thread of scarlet, and thy speech is comely: thy temples are like a piece of a pomegranate within thy locks.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Thy neck is like the tower of David builded for an armoury, whereon there hang a thousand bucklers, all shields of mighty men.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Thy two breasts are like two young roes that are twins, which feed among the lilies.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> Until the day break, and the shadows flee away, I will get me to the mountain of myrrh, and to the hill of frankincense.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> Thou art all fair, my love; there is no spot in thee.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Come with me from Lebanon, my spouse, with me from Lebanon: look from the top of Amana, from the top of Shenir and Hermon, from the lions' dens, from the mountains of the leopards.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Thou hast ravished my heart, my sister, my spouse; thou hast ravished my heart with one of thine eyes, with one chain of thy neck.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> How fair is thy love, my sister, my spouse! how much better is thy love than wine! and the smell of thine ointments than all spices!</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Thy lips, O my spouse, drop as the honeycomb: honey and milk are under thy tongue; and the smell of thy garments is like the smell of Lebanon.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> A garden inclosed is my sister, my spouse; a spring shut up, a fountain sealed.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Thy plants are an orchard of pomegranates, with pleasant fruits; camphire, with spikenard,</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> Spikenard and saffron; calamus and cinnamon, with all trees of frankincense; myrrh and aloes, with all the chief spices:</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> A fountain of gardens, a well of living waters, and streams from Lebanon.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> Awake, O north wind; and come, thou south; blow upon my garden, that the spices thereof may flow out. Let my beloved come into his garden, and eat his pleasant fruits.</p>
