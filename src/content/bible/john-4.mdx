## John 4

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> When therefore the Lord knew how the Pharisees had heard that Jesus made and baptized more disciples than John,</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> (Though Jesus himself baptized not, but his disciples,)</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> He left Judaea, and departed again into Galilee.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> And he must needs go through Samaria.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Then cometh he to a city of Samaria, which is called Sychar, near to the parcel of ground that Jacob gave to his son Joseph.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> Now Jacob's well was there. Jesus therefore, being wearied with his journey, sat thus on the well: and it was about the sixth hour.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> There cometh a woman of Samaria to draw water: Jesus saith unto her, Give me to drink.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> (For his disciples were gone away unto the city to buy meat.)</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Then saith the woman of Samaria unto him, How is it that thou, being a Jew, askest drink of me, which am a woman of Samaria? for the Jews have no dealings with the Samaritans.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Jesus answered and said unto her, If thou knewest the gift of God, and who it is that saith to thee, Give me to drink; thou wouldest have asked of him, and he would have given thee living water.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> The woman saith unto him, Sir, thou hast nothing to draw with, and the well is deep: from whence then hast thou that living water?</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> Art thou greater than our father Jacob, which gave us the well, and drank thereof himself, and his children, and his cattle?</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Jesus answered and said unto her, Whosoever drinketh of this water shall thirst again:</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> But whosoever drinketh of the water that I shall give him shall never thirst; but the water that I shall give him shall be in him a well of water springing up into everlasting life.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> The woman saith unto him, Sir, give me this water, that I thirst not, neither come hither to draw.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> Jesus saith unto her, Go, call thy husband, and come hither.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> The woman answered and said, I have no husband. Jesus said unto her, Thou hast well said, I have no husband:</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> For thou hast had five husbands; and he whom thou now hast is not thy husband: in that saidst thou truly.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> The woman saith unto him, Sir, I perceive that thou art a prophet.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> Our fathers worshipped in this mountain; and ye say, that in Jerusalem is the place where men ought to worship.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> Jesus saith unto her, Woman, believe me, the hour cometh, when ye shall neither in this mountain, nor yet at Jerusalem, worship the Father.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> Ye worship ye know not what: we know what we worship: for salvation is of the Jews.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> But the hour cometh, and now is, when the true worshippers shall worship the Father in spirit and in truth: for the Father seeketh such to worship him.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> God is a Spirit: and they that worship him must worship him in spirit and in truth.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> The woman saith unto him, I know that Messias cometh, which is called Christ: when he is come, he will tell us all things.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> Jesus saith unto her, I that speak unto thee am he.</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> And upon this came his disciples, and marvelled that he talked with the woman: yet no man said, What seekest thou? or, Why talkest thou with her?</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> The woman then left her waterpot, and went her way into the city, and saith to the men,</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> Come, see a man, which told me all things that ever I did: is not this the Christ?</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> Then they went out of the city, and came unto him.</p>

<p id="v31" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">31</span> In the mean while his disciples prayed him, saying, Master, eat.</p>

<p id="v32" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">32</span> But he said unto them, I have meat to eat that ye know not of.</p>

<p id="v33" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">33</span> Therefore said the disciples one to another, Hath any man brought him ought to eat?</p>

<p id="v34" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">34</span> Jesus saith unto them, My meat is to do the will of him that sent me, and to finish his work.</p>

<p id="v35" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">35</span> Say not ye, There are yet four months, and then cometh harvest? behold, I say unto you, Lift up your eyes, and look on the fields; for they are white already to harvest.</p>

<p id="v36" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">36</span> And he that reapeth receiveth wages, and gathereth fruit unto life eternal: that both he that soweth and he that reapeth may rejoice together.</p>

<p id="v37" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">37</span> And herein is that saying true, One soweth, and another reapeth.</p>

<p id="v38" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">38</span> I sent you to reap that whereon ye bestowed no labour: other men laboured, and ye are entered into their labours.</p>

<p id="v39" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">39</span> And many of the Samaritans of that city believed on him for the saying of the woman, which testified, He told me all that ever I did.</p>

<p id="v40" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">40</span> So when the Samaritans were come unto him, they besought him that he would tarry with them: and he abode there two days.</p>

<p id="v41" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">41</span> And many more believed because of his own word;</p>

<p id="v42" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">42</span> And said unto the woman, Now we believe, not because of thy saying: for we have heard him ourselves, and know that this is indeed the Christ, the Saviour of the world.</p>

<p id="v43" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">43</span> Now after two days he departed thence, and went into Galilee.</p>

<p id="v44" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">44</span> For Jesus himself testified, that a prophet hath no honour in his own country.</p>

<p id="v45" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">45</span> Then when he was come into Galilee, the Galilaeans received him, having seen all the things that he did at Jerusalem at the feast: for they also went unto the feast.</p>

<p id="v46" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">46</span> So Jesus came again into Cana of Galilee, where he made the water wine. And there was a certain nobleman, whose son was sick at Capernaum.</p>

<p id="v47" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">47</span> When he heard that Jesus was come out of Judaea into Galilee, he went unto him, and besought him that he would come down, and heal his son: for he was at the point of death.</p>

<p id="v48" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">48</span> Then said Jesus unto him, Except ye see signs and wonders, ye will not believe.</p>

<p id="v49" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">49</span> The nobleman saith unto him, Sir, come down ere my child die.</p>

<p id="v50" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">50</span> Jesus saith unto him, Go thy way; thy son liveth. And the man believed the word that Jesus had spoken unto him, and he went his way.</p>

<p id="v51" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">51</span> And as he was now going down, his servants met him, and told him, saying, Thy son liveth.</p>

<p id="v52" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">52</span> Then enquired he of them the hour when he began to amend. And they said unto him, Yesterday at the seventh hour the fever left him.</p>

<p id="v53" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">53</span> So the father knew that it was at the same hour, in the which Jesus said unto him, Thy son liveth: and himself believed, and his whole house.</p>

<p id="v54" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">54</span> This is again the second miracle that Jesus did, when he was come out of Judaea into Galilee.</p>
