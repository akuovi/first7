## Job 14

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Man that is born of a woman is of few days, and full of trouble.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> He cometh forth like a flower, and is cut down: he fleeth also as a shadow, and continueth not.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> And dost thou open thine eyes upon such an one, and bringest me into judgment with thee?</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Who can bring a clean thing out of an unclean? not one.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Seeing his days are determined, the number of his months are with thee, thou hast appointed his bounds that he cannot pass;</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> Turn from him, that he may rest, till he shall accomplish, as an hireling, his day.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> For there is hope of a tree, if it be cut down, that it will sprout again, and that the tender branch thereof will not cease.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Though the root thereof wax old in the earth, and the stock thereof die in the ground;</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Yet through the scent of water it will bud, and bring forth boughs like a plant.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> But man dieth, and wasteth away: yea, man giveth up the ghost, and where is he?</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> As the waters fail from the sea, and the flood decayeth and drieth up:</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> So man lieth down, and riseth not: till the heavens be no more, they shall not awake, nor be raised out of their sleep.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> O that thou wouldest hide me in the grave, that thou wouldest keep me secret, until thy wrath be past, that thou wouldest appoint me a set time, and remember me!</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> If a man die, shall he live again? all the days of my appointed time will I wait, till my change come.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> Thou shalt call, and I will answer thee: thou wilt have a desire to the work of thine hands.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> For now thou numberest my steps: dost thou not watch over my sin?</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> My transgression is sealed up in a bag, and thou sewest up mine iniquity.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> And surely the mountain falling cometh to nought, and the rock is removed out of his place.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> The waters wear the stones: thou washest away the things which grow out of the dust of the earth; and thou destroyest the hope of man.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> Thou prevailest for ever against him, and he passeth: thou changest his countenance, and sendest him away.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> His sons come to honour, and he knoweth it not; and they are brought low, but he perceiveth it not of them.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> But his flesh upon him shall have pain, and his soul within him shall mourn.</p>
