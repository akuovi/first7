## Acts 8

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> And Saul was consenting unto his death. And at that time there was a great persecution against the church which was at Jerusalem; and they were all scattered abroad throughout the regions of Judaea and Samaria, except the apostles.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> And devout men carried Stephen to his burial, and made great lamentation over him.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> As for Saul, he made havock of the church, entering into every house, and haling men and women committed them to prison.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Therefore they that were scattered abroad went every where preaching the word.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Then Philip went down to the city of Samaria, and preached Christ unto them.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> And the people with one accord gave heed unto those things which Philip spake, hearing and seeing the miracles which he did.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> For unclean spirits, crying with loud voice, came out of many that were possessed with them: and many taken with palsies, and that were lame, were healed.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> And there was great joy in that city.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> But there was a certain man, called Simon, which beforetime in the same city used sorcery, and bewitched the people of Samaria, giving out that himself was some great one:</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> To whom they all gave heed, from the least to the greatest, saying, This man is the great power of God.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> And to him they had regard, because that of long time he had bewitched them with sorceries.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> But when they believed Philip preaching the things concerning the kingdom of God, and the name of Jesus Christ, they were baptized, both men and women.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Then Simon himself believed also: and when he was baptized, he continued with Philip, and wondered, beholding the miracles and signs which were done.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> Now when the apostles which were at Jerusalem heard that Samaria had received the word of God, they sent unto them Peter and John:</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> Who, when they were come down, prayed for them, that they might receive the Holy Ghost:</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> (For as yet he was fallen upon none of them: only they were baptized in the name of the Lord Jesus.)</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> Then laid they their hands on them, and they received the Holy Ghost.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> And when Simon saw that through laying on of the apostles' hands the Holy Ghost was given, he offered them money,</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> Saying, Give me also this power, that on whomsoever I lay hands, he may receive the Holy Ghost.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> But Peter said unto him, Thy money perish with thee, because thou hast thought that the gift of God may be purchased with money.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> Thou hast neither part nor lot in this matter: for thy heart is not right in the sight of God.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> Repent therefore of this thy wickedness, and pray God, if perhaps the thought of thine heart may be forgiven thee.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> For I perceive that thou art in the gall of bitterness, and in the bond of iniquity.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> Then answered Simon, and said, Pray ye to the Lord for me, that none of these things which ye have spoken come upon me.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> And they, when they had testified and preached the word of the Lord, returned to Jerusalem, and preached the gospel in many villages of the Samaritans.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> And the angel of the Lord spake unto Philip, saying, Arise, and go toward the south unto the way that goeth down from Jerusalem unto Gaza, which is desert.</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> And he arose and went: and, behold, a man of Ethiopia, an eunuch of great authority under Candace queen of the Ethiopians, who had the charge of all her treasure, and had come to Jerusalem for to worship,</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> Was returning, and sitting in his chariot read Esaias the prophet.</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> Then the Spirit said unto Philip, Go near, and join thyself to this chariot.</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> And Philip ran thither to him, and heard him read the prophet Esaias, and said, Understandest thou what thou readest?</p>

<p id="v31" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">31</span> And he said, How can I, except some man should guide me? And he desired Philip that he would come up and sit with him.</p>

<p id="v32" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">32</span> The place of the scripture which he read was this, He was led as a sheep to the slaughter; and like a lamb dumb before his shearer, so opened he not his mouth:</p>

<p id="v33" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">33</span> In his humiliation his judgment was taken away: and who shall declare his generation? for his life is taken from the earth.</p>

<p id="v34" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">34</span> And the eunuch answered Philip, and said, I pray thee, of whom speaketh the prophet this? of himself, or of some other man?</p>

<p id="v35" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">35</span> Then Philip opened his mouth, and began at the same scripture, and preached unto him Jesus.</p>

<p id="v36" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">36</span> And as they went on their way, they came unto a certain water: and the eunuch said, See, here is water; what doth hinder me to be baptized?</p>

<p id="v37" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">37</span> And Philip said, If thou believest with all thine heart, thou mayest. And he answered and said, I believe that Jesus Christ is the Son of God.</p>

<p id="v38" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">38</span> And he commanded the chariot to stand still: and they went down both into the water, both Philip and the eunuch; and he baptized him.</p>

<p id="v39" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">39</span> And when they were come up out of the water, the Spirit of the Lord caught away Philip, that the eunuch saw him no more: and he went on his way rejoicing.</p>

<p id="v40" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">40</span> But Philip was found at Azotus: and passing through he preached in all the cities, till he came to Caesarea.</p>
