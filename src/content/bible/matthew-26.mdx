## Matthew 26

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> And it came to pass, when Jesus had finished all these sayings, he said unto his disciples,</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> Ye know that after two days is the feast of the passover, and the Son of man is betrayed to be crucified.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> Then assembled together the chief priests, and the scribes, and the elders of the people, unto the palace of the high priest, who was called Caiaphas,</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> And consulted that they might take Jesus by subtilty, and kill him.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> But they said, Not on the feast day, lest there be an uproar among the people.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> Now when Jesus was in Bethany, in the house of Simon the leper,</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> There came unto him a woman having an alabaster box of very precious ointment, and poured it on his head, as he sat at meat.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> But when his disciples saw it, they had indignation, saying, To what purpose is this waste?</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> For this ointment might have been sold for much, and given to the poor.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> When Jesus understood it, he said unto them, Why trouble ye the woman? for she hath wrought a good work upon me.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> For ye have the poor always with you; but me ye have not always.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> For in that she hath poured this ointment on my body, she did it for my burial.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Verily I say unto you, Wheresoever this gospel shall be preached in the whole world, there shall also this, that this woman hath done, be told for a memorial of her.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> Then one of the twelve, called Judas Iscariot, went unto the chief priests,</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> And said unto them, What will ye give me, and I will deliver him unto you? And they covenanted with him for thirty pieces of silver.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> And from that time he sought opportunity to betray him.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> Now the first day of the feast of unleavened bread the disciples came to Jesus, saying unto him, Where wilt thou that we prepare for thee to eat the passover?</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> And he said, Go into the city to such a man, and say unto him, The Master saith, My time is at hand; I will keep the passover at thy house with my disciples.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> And the disciples did as Jesus had appointed them; and they made ready the passover.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> Now when the even was come, he sat down with the twelve.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> And as they did eat, he said, Verily I say unto you, that one of you shall betray me.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> And they were exceeding sorrowful, and began every one of them to say unto him, Lord, is it I?</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> And he answered and said, He that dippeth his hand with me in the dish, the same shall betray me.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> The Son of man goeth as it is written of him: but woe unto that man by whom the Son of man is betrayed! it had been good for that man if he had not been born.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> Then Judas, which betrayed him, answered and said, Master, is it I? He said unto him, Thou hast said.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> And as they were eating, Jesus took bread, and blessed it, and brake it, and gave it to the disciples, and said, Take, eat; this is my body.</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> And he took the cup, and gave thanks, and gave it to them, saying, Drink ye all of it;</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> For this is my blood of the new testament, which is shed for many for the remission of sins.</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> But I say unto you, I will not drink henceforth of this fruit of the vine, until that day when I drink it new with you in my Father's kingdom.</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> And when they had sung an hymn, they went out into the mount of Olives.</p>

<p id="v31" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">31</span> Then saith Jesus unto them, All ye shall be offended because of me this night: for it is written, I will smite the shepherd, and the sheep of the flock shall be scattered abroad.</p>

<p id="v32" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">32</span> But after I am risen again, I will go before you into Galilee.</p>

<p id="v33" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">33</span> Peter answered and said unto him, Though all men shall be offended because of thee, yet will I never be offended.</p>

<p id="v34" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">34</span> Jesus said unto him, Verily I say unto thee, That this night, before the cock crow, thou shalt deny me thrice.</p>

<p id="v35" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">35</span> Peter said unto him, Though I should die with thee, yet will I not deny thee. Likewise also said all the disciples.</p>

<p id="v36" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">36</span> Then cometh Jesus with them unto a place called Gethsemane, and saith unto the disciples, Sit ye here, while I go and pray yonder.</p>

<p id="v37" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">37</span> And he took with him Peter and the two sons of Zebedee, and began to be sorrowful and very heavy.</p>

<p id="v38" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">38</span> Then saith he unto them, My soul is exceeding sorrowful, even unto death: tarry ye here, and watch with me.</p>

<p id="v39" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">39</span> And he went a little further, and fell on his face, and prayed, saying, O my Father, if it be possible, let this cup pass from me: nevertheless not as I will, but as thou wilt.</p>

<p id="v40" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">40</span> And he cometh unto the disciples, and findeth them asleep, and saith unto Peter, What, could ye not watch with me one hour?</p>

<p id="v41" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">41</span> Watch and pray, that ye enter not into temptation: the spirit indeed is willing, but the flesh is weak.</p>

<p id="v42" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">42</span> He went away again the second time, and prayed, saying, O my Father, if this cup may not pass away from me, except I drink it, thy will be done.</p>

<p id="v43" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">43</span> And he came and found them asleep again: for their eyes were heavy.</p>

<p id="v44" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">44</span> And he left them, and went away again, and prayed the third time, saying the same words.</p>

<p id="v45" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">45</span> Then cometh he to his disciples, and saith unto them, Sleep on now, and take your rest: behold, the hour is at hand, and the Son of man is betrayed into the hands of sinners.</p>

<p id="v46" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">46</span> Rise, let us be going: behold, he is at hand that doth betray me.</p>

<p id="v47" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">47</span> And while he yet spake, lo, Judas, one of the twelve, came, and with him a great multitude with swords and staves, from the chief priests and elders of the people.</p>

<p id="v48" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">48</span> Now he that betrayed him gave them a sign, saying, Whomsoever I shall kiss, that same is he: hold him fast.</p>

<p id="v49" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">49</span> And forthwith he came to Jesus, and said, Hail, master; and kissed him.</p>

<p id="v50" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">50</span> And Jesus said unto him, Friend, wherefore art thou come? Then came they, and laid hands on Jesus, and took him.</p>

<p id="v51" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">51</span> And, behold, one of them which were with Jesus stretched out his hand, and drew his sword, and struck a servant of the high priest's, and smote off his ear.</p>

<p id="v52" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">52</span> Then said Jesus unto him, Put up again thy sword into his place: for all they that take the sword shall perish with the sword.</p>

<p id="v53" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">53</span> Thinkest thou that I cannot now pray to my Father, and he shall presently give me more than twelve legions of angels?</p>

<p id="v54" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">54</span> But how then shall the scriptures be fulfilled, that thus it must be?</p>

<p id="v55" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">55</span> In that same hour said Jesus to the multitudes, Are ye come out as against a thief with swords and staves for to take me? I sat daily with you teaching in the temple, and ye laid no hold on me.</p>

<p id="v56" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">56</span> But all this was done, that the scriptures of the prophets might be fulfilled. Then all the disciples forsook him, and fled.</p>

<p id="v57" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">57</span> And they that had laid hold on Jesus led him away to Caiaphas the high priest, where the scribes and the elders were assembled.</p>

<p id="v58" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">58</span> But Peter followed him afar off unto the high priest's palace, and went in, and sat with the servants, to see the end.</p>

<p id="v59" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">59</span> Now the chief priests, and elders, and all the council, sought false witness against Jesus, to put him to death;</p>

<p id="v60" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">60</span> But found none: yea, though many false witnesses came, yet found they none. At the last came two false witnesses,</p>

<p id="v61" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">61</span> And said, This fellow said, I am able to destroy the temple of God, and to build it in three days.</p>

<p id="v62" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">62</span> And the high priest arose, and said unto him, Answerest thou nothing? what is it which these witness against thee?</p>

<p id="v63" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">63</span> But Jesus held his peace. And the high priest answered and said unto him, I adjure thee by the living God, that thou tell us whether thou be the Christ, the Son of God.</p>

<p id="v64" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">64</span> Jesus saith unto him, Thou hast said: nevertheless I say unto you, Hereafter shall ye see the Son of man sitting on the right hand of power, and coming in the clouds of heaven.</p>

<p id="v65" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">65</span> Then the high priest rent his clothes, saying, He hath spoken blasphemy; what further need have we of witnesses? behold, now ye have heard his blasphemy.</p>

<p id="v66" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">66</span> What think ye? They answered and said, He is guilty of death.</p>

<p id="v67" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">67</span> Then did they spit in his face, and buffeted him; and others smote him with the palms of their hands,</p>

<p id="v68" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">68</span> Saying, Prophesy unto us, thou Christ, Who is he that smote thee?</p>

<p id="v69" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">69</span> Now Peter sat without in the palace: and a damsel came unto him, saying, Thou also wast with Jesus of Galilee.</p>

<p id="v70" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">70</span> But he denied before them all, saying, I know not what thou sayest.</p>

<p id="v71" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">71</span> And when he was gone out into the porch, another maid saw him, and said unto them that were there, This fellow was also with Jesus of Nazareth.</p>

<p id="v72" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">72</span> And again he denied with an oath, I do not know the man.</p>

<p id="v73" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">73</span> And after a while came unto him they that stood by, and said to Peter, Surely thou also art one of them; for thy speech bewrayeth thee.</p>

<p id="v74" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">74</span> Then began he to curse and to swear, saying, I know not the man. And immediately the cock crew.</p>

<p id="v75" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">75</span> And Peter remembered the word of Jesus, which said unto him, Before the cock crow, thou shalt deny me thrice. And he went out, and wept bitterly.</p>
