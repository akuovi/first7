## James 1

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> James, a servant of God and of the Lord Jesus Christ, to the twelve tribes which are scattered abroad, greeting.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> My brethren, count it all joy when ye fall into divers temptations;</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> Knowing this, that the trying of your faith worketh patience.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> But let patience have her perfect work, that ye may be perfect and entire, wanting nothing.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> If any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God, that giveth to all men liberally, and upbraideth not; and it shall be given him.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> But let him ask in faith, nothing wavering. For he that wavereth is like a wave of the sea driven with the wind and tossed.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> For let not that man think that he shall receive any thing of the Lord.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> A double minded man is unstable in all his ways.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Let the brother of low degree rejoice in that he is exalted:</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> But the rich, in that he is made low: because as the flower of the grass he shall pass away.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> For the sun is no sooner risen with a burning heat, but it withereth the grass, and the flower thereof falleth, and the grace of the fashion of it perisheth: so also shall the rich man fade away in his ways.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> Blessed is the man that endureth temptation: for when he is tried, he shall receive the crown of life, which the Lord hath promised to them that love him.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Let no man say when he is tempted, I am tempted of God: for God cannot be tempted with evil, neither tempteth he any man:</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> But every man is tempted, when he is drawn away of his own lust, and enticed.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> Then when lust hath conceived, it bringeth forth sin: and sin, when it is finished, bringeth forth death.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> Do not err, my beloved brethren.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> Every good gift and every perfect gift is from above, and cometh down from the Father of lights, with whom is no variableness, neither shadow of turning.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> Of his own will begat he us with the word of truth, that we should be a kind of firstfruits of his creatures.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> Wherefore, my beloved brethren, let every man be swift to hear, slow to speak, slow to wrath:</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> For the wrath of man worketh not the righteousness of God.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> Wherefore lay apart all filthiness and superfluity of naughtiness, and receive with meekness the engrafted word, which is able to save your souls.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> But be ye doers of the word, and not hearers only, deceiving your own selves.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> For if any be a hearer of the word, and not a doer, he is like unto a man beholding his natural face in a glass:</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> For he beholdeth himself, and goeth his way, and straightway forgetteth what manner of man he was.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> But whoso looketh into the perfect law of liberty, and continueth therein, he being not a forgetful hearer, but a doer of the work, this man shall be blessed in his deed.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> If any man among you seem to be religious, and bridleth not his tongue, but deceiveth his own heart, this man's religion is vain.</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> Pure religion and undefiled before God and the Father is this, To visit the fatherless and widows in their affliction, and to keep himself unspotted from the world.</p>
