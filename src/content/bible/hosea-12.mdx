## Hosea 12

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Ephraim feedeth on wind, and followeth after the east wind: he daily increaseth lies and desolation; and they do make a covenant with the Assyrians, and oil is carried into Egypt.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> The LORD hath also a controversy with Judah, and will punish Jacob according to his ways; according to his doings will he recompense him.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> He took his brother by the heel in the womb, and by his strength he had power with God:</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Yea, he had power over the angel, and prevailed: he wept, and made supplication unto him: he found him in Beth-el, and there he spake with us;</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Even the LORD God of hosts; the LORD is his memorial.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> Therefore turn thou to thy God: keep mercy and judgment, and wait on thy God continually.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> He is a merchant, the balances of deceit are in his hand: he loveth to oppress.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> And Ephraim said, Yet I am become rich, I have found me out substance: in all my labours they shall find none iniquity in me that were sin.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> And I that am the LORD thy God from the land of Egypt will yet make thee to dwell in tabernacles, as in the days of the solemn feast.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> I have also spoken by the prophets, and I have multiplied visions, and used similitudes, by the ministry of the prophets.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Is there iniquity in Gilead? surely they are vanity: they sacrifice bullocks in Gilgal; yea, their altars are as heaps in the furrows of the fields.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> And Jacob fled into the country of Syria, and Israel served for a wife, and for a wife he kept sheep.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> And by a prophet the LORD brought Israel out of Egypt, and by a prophet was he preserved.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> Ephraim provoked him to anger most bitterly: therefore shall he leave his blood upon him, and his reproach shall his Lord return unto him.</p>
