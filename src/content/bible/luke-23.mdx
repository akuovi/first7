## Luke 23

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> And the whole multitude of them arose, and led him unto Pilate.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> And they began to accuse him, saying, We found this fellow perverting the nation, and forbidding to give tribute to Caesar, saying that he himself is Christ a King.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> And Pilate asked him, saying, Art thou the King of the Jews? And he answered him and said, Thou sayest it.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Then said Pilate to the chief priests and to the people, I find no fault in this man.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> And they were the more fierce, saying, He stirreth up the people, teaching throughout all Jewry, beginning from Galilee to this place.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> When Pilate heard of Galilee, he asked whether the man were a Galilaean.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> And as soon as he knew that he belonged unto Herod's jurisdiction, he sent him to Herod, who himself also was at Jerusalem at that time.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> And when Herod saw Jesus, he was exceeding glad: for he was desirous to see him of a long season, because he had heard many things of him; and he hoped to have seen some miracle done by him.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Then he questioned with him in many words; but he answered him nothing.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> And the chief priests and scribes stood and vehemently accused him.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> And Herod with his men of war set him at nought, and mocked him, and arrayed him in a gorgeous robe, and sent him again to Pilate.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> And the same day Pilate and Herod were made friends together: for before they were at enmity between themselves.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> And Pilate, when he had called together the chief priests and the rulers and the people,</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> Said unto them, Ye have brought this man unto me, as one that perverteth the people: and, behold, I, having examined him before you, have found no fault in this man touching those things whereof ye accuse him:</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> No, nor yet Herod: for I sent you to him; and, lo, nothing worthy of death is done unto him.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> I will therefore chastise him, and release him.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> (For of necessity he must release one unto them at the feast.)</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> And they cried out all at once, saying, Away with this man, and release unto us Barabbas:</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> (Who for a certain sedition made in the city, and for murder, was cast into prison.)</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> Pilate therefore, willing to release Jesus, spake again to them.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> But they cried, saying, Crucify him, crucify him.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> And he said unto them the third time, Why, what evil hath he done? I have found no cause of death in him: I will therefore chastise him, and let him go.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> And they were instant with loud voices, requiring that he might be crucified. And the voices of them and of the chief priests prevailed.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> And Pilate gave sentence that it should be as they required.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> And he released unto them him that for sedition and murder was cast into prison, whom they had desired; but he delivered Jesus to their will.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> And as they led him away, they laid hold upon one Simon, a Cyrenian, coming out of the country, and on him they laid the cross, that he might bear it after Jesus.</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> And there followed him a great company of people, and of women, which also bewailed and lamented him.</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> But Jesus turning unto them said, Daughters of Jerusalem, weep not for me, but weep for yourselves, and for your children.</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> For, behold, the days are coming, in the which they shall say, Blessed are the barren, and the wombs that never bare, and the paps which never gave suck.</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> Then shall they begin to say to the mountains, Fall on us; and to the hills, Cover us.</p>

<p id="v31" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">31</span> For if they do these things in a green tree, what shall be done in the dry?</p>

<p id="v32" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">32</span> And there were also two other, malefactors, led with him to be put to death.</p>

<p id="v33" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">33</span> And when they were come to the place, which is called Calvary, there they crucified him, and the malefactors, one on the right hand, and the other on the left.</p>

<p id="v34" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">34</span> Then said Jesus, Father, forgive them; for they know not what they do. And they parted his raiment, and cast lots.</p>

<p id="v35" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">35</span> And the people stood beholding. And the rulers also with them derided him, saying, He saved others; let him save himself, if he be Christ, the chosen of God.</p>

<p id="v36" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">36</span> And the soldiers also mocked him, coming to him, and offering him vinegar,</p>

<p id="v37" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">37</span> And saying, If thou be the king of the Jews, save thyself.</p>

<p id="v38" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">38</span> And a superscription also was written over him in letters of Greek, and Latin, and Hebrew, THIS IS THE KING OF THE JEWS.</p>

<p id="v39" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">39</span> And one of the malefactors which were hanged railed on him, saying, If thou be Christ, save thyself and us.</p>

<p id="v40" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">40</span> But the other answering rebuked him, saying, Dost not thou fear God, seeing thou art in the same condemnation?</p>

<p id="v41" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">41</span> And we indeed justly; for we receive the due reward of our deeds: but this man hath done nothing amiss.</p>

<p id="v42" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">42</span> And he said unto Jesus, Lord, remember me when thou comest into thy kingdom.</p>

<p id="v43" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">43</span> And Jesus said unto him, Verily I say unto thee, To day shalt thou be with me in paradise.</p>

<p id="v44" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">44</span> And it was about the sixth hour, and there was a darkness over all the earth until the ninth hour.</p>

<p id="v45" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">45</span> And the sun was darkened, and the veil of the temple was rent in the midst.</p>

<p id="v46" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">46</span> And when Jesus had cried with a loud voice, he said, Father, into thy hands I commend my spirit: and having said thus, he gave up the ghost.</p>

<p id="v47" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">47</span> Now when the centurion saw what was done, he glorified God, saying, Certainly this was a righteous man.</p>

<p id="v48" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">48</span> And all the people that came together to that sight, beholding the things which were done, smote their breasts, and returned.</p>

<p id="v49" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">49</span> And all his acquaintance, and the women that followed him from Galilee, stood afar off, beholding these things.</p>

<p id="v50" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">50</span> And, behold, there was a man named Joseph, a counsellor; and he was a good man, and a just:</p>

<p id="v51" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">51</span> (The same had not consented to the counsel and deed of them;) he was of Arimathaea, a city of the Jews: who also himself waited for the kingdom of God.</p>

<p id="v52" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">52</span> This man went unto Pilate, and begged the body of Jesus.</p>

<p id="v53" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">53</span> And he took it down, and wrapped it in linen, and laid it in a sepulchre that was hewn in stone, wherein never man before was laid.</p>

<p id="v54" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">54</span> And that day was the preparation, and the sabbath drew on.</p>

<p id="v55" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">55</span> And the women also, which came with him from Galilee, followed after, and beheld the sepulchre, and how his body was laid.</p>

<p id="v56" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">56</span> And they returned, and prepared spices and ointments; and rested the sabbath day according to the commandment.</p>
