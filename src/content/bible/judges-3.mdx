## Judges 3

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Now these are the nations which the LORD left, to prove Israel by them, even as many of Israel as had not known all the wars of Canaan;</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> Only that the generations of the children of Israel might know, to teach them war, at the least such as before knew nothing thereof;</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> Namely, five lords of the Philistines, and all the Canaanites, and the Sidonians, and the Hivites that dwelt in mount Lebanon, from mount Baal-hermon unto the entering in of Hamath.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> And they were to prove Israel by them, to know whether they would hearken unto the commandments of the LORD, which he commanded their fathers by the hand of Moses.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> And the children of Israel dwelt among the Canaanites, Hittites, and Amorites, and Perizzites, and Hivites, and Jebusites:</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> And they took their daughters to be their wives, and gave their daughters to their sons, and served their gods.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> And the children of Israel did evil in the sight of the LORD, and forgat the LORD their God, and served Baalim and the groves.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Therefore the anger of the LORD was hot against Israel, and he sold them into the hand of Chushan-rishathaim king of Mesopotamia: and the children of Israel served Chushan-rishathaim eight years.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> And when the children of Israel cried unto the LORD, the LORD raised up a deliverer to the children of Israel, who delivered them, even Othniel the son of Kenaz, Caleb's younger brother.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> And the Spirit of the LORD came upon him, and he judged Israel, and went out to war: and the LORD delivered Chushan-rishathaim king of Mesopotamia into his hand; and his hand prevailed against Chushan-rishathaim.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> And the land had rest forty years. And Othniel the son of Kenaz died.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> And the children of Israel did evil again in the sight of the LORD: and the LORD strengthened Eglon the king of Moab against Israel, because they had done evil in the sight of the LORD.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> And he gathered unto him the children of Ammon and Amalek, and went and smote Israel, and possessed the city of palm trees.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> So the children of Israel served Eglon the king of Moab eighteen years.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> But when the children of Israel cried unto the LORD, the LORD raised them up a deliverer, Ehud the son of Gera, a Benjamite, a man lefthanded: and by him the children of Israel sent a present unto Eglon the king of Moab.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> But Ehud made him a dagger which had two edges, of a cubit length; and he did gird it under his raiment upon his right thigh.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> And he brought the present unto Eglon king of Moab: and Eglon was a very fat man.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> And when he had made an end to offer the present, he sent away the people that bare the present.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> But he himself turned again from the quarries that were by Gilgal, and said, I have a secret errand unto thee, O king: who said, Keep silence. And all that stood by him went out from him.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> And Ehud came unto him; and he was sitting in a summer parlour, which he had for himself alone. And Ehud said, I have a message from God unto thee. And he arose out of his seat.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> And Ehud put forth his left hand, and took the dagger from his right thigh, and thrust it into his belly:</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> And the haft also went in after the blade; and the fat closed upon the blade, so that he could not draw the dagger out of his belly; and the dirt came out.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> Then Ehud went forth through the porch, and shut the doors of the parlour upon him, and locked them.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> When he was gone out, his servants came; and when they saw that, behold, the doors of the parlour were locked, they said, Surely he covereth his feet in his summer chamber.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> And they tarried till they were ashamed: and, behold, he opened not the doors of the parlour; therefore they took a key, and opened them: and, behold, their lord was fallen down dead on the earth.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> And Ehud escaped while they tarried, and passed beyond the quarries, and escaped unto Seirath.</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> And it came to pass, when he was come, that he blew a trumpet in the mountain of Ephraim, and the children of Israel went down with him from the mount, and he before them.</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> And he said unto them, Follow after me: for the LORD hath delivered your enemies the Moabites into your hand. And they went down after him, and took the fords of Jordan toward Moab, and suffered not a man to pass over.</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> And they slew of Moab at that time about ten thousand men, all lusty, and all men of valour; and there escaped not a man.</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> So Moab was subdued that day under the hand of Israel. And the land had rest fourscore years.</p>

<p id="v31" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">31</span> And after him was Shamgar the son of Anath, which slew of the Philistines six hundred men with an ox goad: and he also delivered Israel.</p>
