## Deuteronomy 24

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> When a man hath taken a wife, and married her, and it come to pass that she find no favour in his eyes, because he hath found some uncleanness in her: then let him write her a bill of divorcement, and give it in her hand, and send her out of his house.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> And when she is departed out of his house, she may go and be another man's wife.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> And if the latter husband hate her, and write her a bill of divorcement, and giveth it in her hand, and sendeth her out of his house; or if the latter husband die, which took her to be his wife;</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Her former husband, which sent her away, may not take her again to be his wife, after that she is defiled; for that is abomination before the LORD: and thou shalt not cause the land to sin, which the LORD thy God giveth thee for an inheritance.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> When a man hath taken a new wife, he shall not go out to war, neither shall he be charged with any business: but he shall be free at home one year, and shall cheer up his wife which he hath taken.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> No man shall take the nether or the upper millstone to pledge: for he taketh a man's life to pledge.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> If a man be found stealing any of his brethren of the children of Israel, and maketh merchandise of him, or selleth him; then that thief shall die; and thou shalt put evil away from among you.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Take heed in the plague of leprosy, that thou observe diligently, and do according to all that the priests the Levites shall teach you: as I commanded them, so ye shall observe to do.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Remember what the LORD thy God did unto Miriam by the way, after that ye were come forth out of Egypt.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> When thou dost lend thy brother any thing, thou shalt not go into his house to fetch his pledge.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Thou shalt stand abroad, and the man to whom thou dost lend shall bring out the pledge abroad unto thee.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> And if the man be poor, thou shalt not sleep with his pledge:</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> In any case thou shalt deliver him the pledge again when the sun goeth down, that he may sleep in his own raiment, and bless thee: and it shall be righteousness unto thee before the LORD thy God.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> Thou shalt not oppress an hired servant that is poor and needy, whether he be of thy brethren, or of thy strangers that are in thy land within thy gates:</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> At his day thou shalt give him his hire, neither shall the sun go down upon it; for he is poor, and setteth his heart upon it: lest he cry against thee unto the LORD, and it be sin unto thee.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> The fathers shall not be put to death for the children, neither shall the children be put to death for the fathers: every man shall be put to death for his own sin.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> Thou shalt not pervert the judgment of the stranger, nor of the fatherless; nor take a widow's raiment to pledge:</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> But thou shalt remember that thou wast a bondman in Egypt, and the LORD thy God redeemed thee thence: therefore I command thee to do this thing.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> When thou cuttest down thine harvest in thy field, and hast forgot a sheaf in the field, thou shalt not go again to fetch it: it shall be for the stranger, for the fatherless, and for the widow: that the LORD thy God may bless thee in all the work of thine hands.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> When thou beatest thine olive tree, thou shalt not go over the boughs again: it shall be for the stranger, for the fatherless, and for the widow.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> When thou gatherest the grapes of thy vineyard, thou shalt not glean it afterward: it shall be for the stranger, for the fatherless, and for the widow.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> And thou shalt remember that thou wast a bondman in the land of Egypt: therefore I command thee to do this thing.</p>
