## Job 31

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> I made a covenant with mine eyes; why then should I think upon a maid?</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> For what portion of God is there from above? and what inheritance of the Almighty from on high?</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> Is not destruction to the wicked? and a strange punishment to the workers of iniquity?</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Doth not he see my ways, and count all my steps?</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> If I have walked with vanity, or if my foot hath hasted to deceit;</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> Let me be weighed in an even balance, that God may know mine integrity.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> If my step hath turned out of the way, and mine heart walked after mine eyes, and if any blot hath cleaved to mine hands;</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Then let me sow, and let another eat; yea, let my offspring be rooted out.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> If mine heart have been deceived by a woman, or if I have laid wait at my neighbour's door;</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Then let my wife grind unto another, and let others bow down upon her.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> For this is an heinous crime; yea, it is an iniquity to be punished by the judges.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> For it is a fire that consumeth to destruction, and would root out all mine increase.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> If I did despise the cause of my manservant or of my maidservant, when they contended with me;</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> What then shall I do when God riseth up? and when he visiteth, what shall I answer him?</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> Did not he that made me in the womb make him? and did not one fashion us in the womb?</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> If I have withheld the poor from their desire, or have caused the eyes of the widow to fail;</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> Or have eaten my morsel myself alone, and the fatherless hath not eaten thereof;</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> (For from my youth he was brought up with me, as with a father, and I have guided her from my mother's womb;)</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> If I have seen any perish for want of clothing, or any poor without covering;</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> If his loins have not blessed me, and if he were not warmed with the fleece of my sheep;</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> If I have lifted up my hand against the fatherless, when I saw my help in the gate:</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> Then let mine arm fall from my shoulder blade, and mine arm be broken from the bone.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> For destruction from God was a terror to me, and by reason of his highness I could not endure.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> If I have made gold my hope, or have said to the fine gold, Thou art my confidence;</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> If I rejoiced because my wealth was great, and because mine hand had gotten much;</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> If I beheld the sun when it shined, or the moon walking in brightness;</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> And my heart hath been secretly enticed, or my mouth hath kissed my hand:</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> This also were an iniquity to be punished by the judge: for I should have denied the God that is above.</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> If I rejoiced at the destruction of him that hated me, or lifted up myself when evil found him:</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> Neither have I suffered my mouth to sin by wishing a curse to his soul.</p>

<p id="v31" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">31</span> If the men of my tabernacle said not, Oh that we had of his flesh! we cannot be satisfied.</p>

<p id="v32" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">32</span> The stranger did not lodge in the street: but I opened my doors to the traveller.</p>

<p id="v33" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">33</span> If I covered my transgressions as Adam, by hiding mine iniquity in my bosom:</p>

<p id="v34" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">34</span> Did I fear a great multitude, or did the contempt of families terrify me, that I kept silence, and went not out of the door?</p>

<p id="v35" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">35</span> Oh that one would hear me! behold, my desire is, that the Almighty would answer me, and that mine adversary had written a book.</p>

<p id="v36" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">36</span> Surely I would take it upon my shoulder, and bind it as a crown to me.</p>

<p id="v37" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">37</span> I would declare unto him the number of my steps; as a prince would I go near unto him.</p>

<p id="v38" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">38</span> If my land cry against me, or that the furrows likewise thereof complain;</p>

<p id="v39" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">39</span> If I have eaten the fruits thereof without money, or have caused the owners thereof to lose their life:</p>

<p id="v40" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">40</span> Let thistles grow instead of wheat, and cockle instead of barley. The words of Job are ended.</p>
