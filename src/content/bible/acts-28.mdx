## Acts 28

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> And when they were escaped, then they knew that the island was called Melita.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> And the barbarous people shewed us no little kindness: for they kindled a fire, and received us every one, because of the present rain, and because of the cold.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> And when Paul had gathered a bundle of sticks, and laid them on the fire, there came a viper out of the heat, and fastened on his hand.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> And when the barbarians saw the venomous beast hang on his hand, they said among themselves, No doubt this man is a murderer, whom, though he hath escaped the sea, yet vengeance suffereth not to live.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> And he shook off the beast into the fire, and felt no harm.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> Howbeit they looked when he should have swollen, or fallen down dead suddenly: but after they had looked a great while, and saw no harm come to him, they changed their minds, and said that he was a god.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> In the same quarters were possessions of the chief man of the island, whose name was Publius; who received us, and lodged us three days courteously.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> And it came to pass, that the father of Publius lay sick of a fever and of a bloody flux: to whom Paul entered in, and prayed, and laid his hands on him, and healed him.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> So when this was done, others also, which had diseases in the island, came, and were healed:</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Who also honoured us with many honours; and when we departed, they laded us with such things as were necessary.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> And after three months we departed in a ship of Alexandria, which had wintered in the isle, whose sign was Castor and Pollux.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> And landing at Syracuse, we tarried there three days.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> And from thence we fetched a compass, and came to Rhegium: and after one day the south wind blew, and we came the next day to Puteoli:</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> Where we found brethren, and were desired to tarry with them seven days: and so we went toward Rome.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> And from thence, when the brethren heard of us, they came to meet us as far as Appii forum, and The three taverns: whom when Paul saw, he thanked God, and took courage.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> And when we came to Rome, the centurion delivered the prisoners to the captain of the guard: but Paul was suffered to dwell by himself with a soldier that kept him.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> And it came to pass, that after three days Paul called the chief of the Jews together: and when they were come together, he said unto them, Men and brethren, though I have committed nothing against the people, or customs of our fathers, yet was I delivered prisoner from Jerusalem into the hands of the Romans.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> Who, when they had examined me, would have let me go, because there was no cause of death in me.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> But when the Jews spake against it, I was constrained to appeal unto Caesar; not that I had ought to accuse my nation of.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> For this cause therefore have I called for you, to see you, and to speak with you: because that for the hope of Israel I am bound with this chain.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> And they said unto him, We neither received letters out of Judaea concerning thee, neither any of the brethren that came shewed or spake any harm of thee.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> But we desire to hear of thee what thou thinkest: for as concerning this sect, we know that every where it is spoken against.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> And when they had appointed him a day, there came many to him into his lodging; to whom he expounded and testified the kingdom of God, persuading them concerning Jesus, both out of the law of Moses, and out of the prophets, from morning till evening.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> And some believed the things which were spoken, and some believed not.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> And when they agreed not among themselves, they departed, after that Paul had spoken one word, Well spake the Holy Ghost by Esaias the prophet unto our fathers,</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> Saying, Go unto this people, and say, Hearing ye shall hear, and shall not understand; and seeing ye shall see, and not perceive:</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> For the heart of this people is waxed gross, and their ears are dull of hearing, and their eyes have they closed; lest they should see with their eyes, and hear with their ears, and understand with their heart, and should be converted, and I should heal them.</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> Be it known therefore unto you, that the salvation of God is sent unto the Gentiles, and that they will hear it.</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> And when he had said these words, the Jews departed, and had great reasoning among themselves.</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> And Paul dwelt two whole years in his own hired house, and received all that came in unto him,</p>

<p id="v31" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">31</span> Preaching the kingdom of God, and teaching those things which concern the Lord Jesus Christ, with all confidence, no man forbidding him.</p>

<p id="v32" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">32</span> ACTS</p>
