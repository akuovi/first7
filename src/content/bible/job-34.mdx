## Job 34

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Furthermore Elihu answered and said,</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> Hear my words, O ye wise men; and give ear unto me, ye that have knowledge.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> For the ear trieth words, as the mouth tasteth meat.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Let us choose to us judgment: let us know among ourselves what is good.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> For Job hath said, I am righteous: and God hath taken away my judgment.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> Should I lie against my right? my wound is incurable without transgression.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> What man is like Job, who drinketh up scorning like water?</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Which goeth in company with the workers of iniquity, and walketh with wicked men.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> For he hath said, It profiteth a man nothing that he should delight himself with God.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Therefore hearken unto me, ye men of understanding: far be it from God, that he should do wickedness; and from the Almighty, that he should commit iniquity.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> For the work of a man shall he render unto him, and cause every man to find according to his ways.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> Yea, surely God will not do wickedly, neither will the Almighty pervert judgment.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Who hath given him a charge over the earth? or who hath disposed the whole world?</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> If he set his heart upon man, if he gather unto himself his spirit and his breath;</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> All flesh shall perish together, and man shall turn again unto dust.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> If now thou hast understanding, hear this: hearken to the voice of my words.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> Shall even he that hateth right govern? and wilt thou condemn him that is most just?</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> Is it fit to say to a king, Thou art wicked? and to princes, Ye are ungodly?</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> How much less to him that accepteth not the persons of princes, nor regardeth the rich more than the poor? for they all are the work of his hands.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> In a moment shall they die, and the people shall be troubled at midnight, and pass away: and the mighty shall be taken away without hand.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> For his eyes are upon the ways of man, and he seeth all his goings.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> There is no darkness, nor shadow of death, where the workers of iniquity may hide themselves.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> For he will not lay upon man more than right; that he should enter into judgment with God.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> He shall break in pieces mighty men without number, and set others in their stead.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> Therefore he knoweth their works, and he overturneth them in the night, so that they are destroyed.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> He striketh them as wicked men in the open sight of others;</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> Because they turned back from him, and would not consider any of his ways:</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> So that they cause the cry of the poor to come unto him, and he heareth the cry of the afflicted.</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> When he giveth quietness, who then can make trouble? and when he hideth his face, who then can behold him? whether it be done against a nation, or against a man only:</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> That the hypocrite reign not, lest the people be ensnared.</p>

<p id="v31" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">31</span> Surely it is meet to be said unto God, I have borne chastisement, I will not offend any more:</p>

<p id="v32" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">32</span> That which I see not teach thou me: if I have done iniquity, I will do no more.</p>

<p id="v33" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">33</span> Should it be according to thy mind? he will recompense it, whether thou refuse, or whether thou choose; and not I: therefore speak what thou knowest.</p>

<p id="v34" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">34</span> Let men of understanding tell me, and let a wise man hearken unto me.</p>

<p id="v35" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">35</span> Job hath spoken without knowledge, and his words were without wisdom.</p>

<p id="v36" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">36</span> My desire is that Job may be tried unto the end because of his answers for wicked men.</p>

<p id="v37" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">37</span> For he addeth rebellion unto his sin, he clappeth his hands among us, and multiplieth his words against God.</p>
