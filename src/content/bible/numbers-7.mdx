## Numbers 7

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> And it came to pass on the day that Moses had fully set up the tabernacle, and had anointed it, and sanctified it, and all the instruments thereof, both the altar and all the vessels thereof, and had anointed them, and sanctified them;</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> That the princes of Israel, heads of the house of their fathers, who were the princes of the tribes, and were over them that were numbered, offered:</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> And they brought their offering before the LORD, six covered wagons, and twelve oxen; a wagon for two of the princes, and for each one an ox: and they brought them before the tabernacle.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> And the LORD spake unto Moses, saying,</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Take it of them, that they may be to do the service of the tabernacle of the congregation; and thou shalt give them unto the Levites, to every man according to his service.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> And Moses took the wagons and the oxen, and gave them unto the Levites.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> Two wagons and four oxen he gave unto the sons of Gershon, according to their service:</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> And four wagons and eight oxen he gave unto the sons of Merari, according unto their service, under the hand of Ithamar the son of Aaron the priest.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> But unto the sons of Kohath he gave none: because the service of the sanctuary belonging unto them was that they should bear upon their shoulders.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> And the princes offered for dedicating of the altar in the day that it was anointed, even the princes offered their offering before the altar.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> And the LORD said unto Moses, They shall offer their offering, each prince on his day, for the dedicating of the altar.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> And he that offered his offering the first day was Nahshon the son of Amminadab, of the tribe of Judah:</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> And his offering was one silver charger, the weight thereof was an hundred and thirty shekels, one silver bowl of seventy shekels, after the shekel of the sanctuary; both of them were full of fine flour mingled with oil for a meat offering:</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> One spoon of ten shekels of gold, full of incense:</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> One young bullock, one ram, one lamb of the first year, for a burnt offering:</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> One kid of the goats for a sin offering:</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> And for a sacrifice of peace offerings, two oxen, five rams, five he goats, five lambs of the first year: this was the offering of Nahshon the son of Amminadab.</p>
