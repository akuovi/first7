## 1 Samuel 14

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Now it came to pass upon a day, that Jonathan the son of Saul said unto the young man that bare his armour, Come, and let us go over to the Philistines' garrison, that is on the other side. But he told not his father.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> And Saul tarried in the uttermost part of Gibeah under a pomegranate tree which is in Migron: and the people that were with him were about six hundred men;</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> And Ahiah, the son of Ahitub, I-chabod's brother, the son of Phinehas, the son of Eli, the LORD'S priest in Shiloh, wearing an ephod. And the people knew not that Jonathan was gone.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> And between the passages, by which Jonathan sought to go over unto the Philistines' garrison, there was a sharp rock on the one side, and a sharp rock on the other side: and the name of the one was Bozez, and the name of the other Seneh.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> The forefront of the one was situate northward over against Michmash, and the other southward over against Gibeah.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> And Jonathan said to the young man that bare his armour, Come, and let us go over unto the garrison of these uncircumcised: it may be that the LORD will work for us: for there is no restraint to the LORD to save by many or by few.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> And his armourbearer said unto him, Do all that is in thine heart: turn thee; behold, I am with thee according to thy heart.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Then said Jonathan, Behold, we will pass over unto these men, and we will discover ourselves unto them.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> If they say thus unto us, Tarry until we come to you; then we will stand still in our place, and will not go up unto them.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> But if they say thus, Come up unto us; then we will go up: for the LORD hath delivered them into our hand: and this shall be a sign unto us.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> And both of them discovered themselves unto the garrison of the Philistines: and the Philistines said, Behold, the Hebrews come forth out of the holes where they had hid themselves.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> And the men of the garrison answered Jonathan and his armourbearer, and said, Come up to us, and we will shew you a thing. And Jonathan said unto his armourbearer, Come up after me: for the LORD hath delivered them into the hand of Israel.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> And Jonathan climbed up upon his hands and upon his feet, and his armourbearer after him: and they fell before Jonathan; and his armourbearer slew after him.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> And that first slaughter, which Jonathan and his armourbearer made, was about twenty men, within as it were an half acre of land, which a yoke of oxen might plow.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> And there was trembling in the host, in the field, and among all the people: the garrison, and the spoilers, they also trembled, and the earth quaked: so it was a very great trembling.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> And the watchmen of Saul in Gibeah of Benjamin looked; and, behold, the multitude melted away, and they went on beating down one another.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> Then said Saul unto the people that were with him, Number now, and see who is gone from us. And when they had numbered, behold, Jonathan and his armourbearer were not there.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> And Saul said unto Ahiah, Bring hither the ark of God. For the ark of God was at that time with the children of Israel.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> And it came to pass, while Saul talked unto the priest, that the noise that was in the host of the Philistines went on and increased: and Saul said unto the priest, Withdraw thine hand.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> And Saul and all the people that were with him assembled themselves, and they came to the battle: and, behold, every man's sword was against his fellow, and there was a very great discomfiture.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> Moreover the Hebrews that were with the Philistines before that time, which went up with them into the camp from the country round about, even they also turned to be with the Israelites that were with Saul and Jonathan.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> Likewise all the men of Israel which had hid themselves in mount Ephraim, when they heard that the Philistines fled, even they also followed hard after them in the battle.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> So the LORD saved Israel that day: and the battle passed over unto Beth-aven.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> And the men of Israel were distressed that day: for Saul had adjured the people, saying, Cursed be the man that eateth any food until evening, that I may be avenged on mine enemies. So none of the people tasted any food.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> And all they of the land came to a wood; and there was honey upon the ground.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> And when the people were come into the wood, behold, the honey dropped; but no man put his hand to his mouth: for the people feared the oath.</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> But Jonathan heard not when his father charged the people with the oath: wherefore he put forth the end of the rod that was in his hand, and dipped it in an honeycomb, and put his hand to his mouth; and his eyes were enlightened.</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> Then answered one of the people, and said, Thy father straitly charged the people with an oath, saying, Cursed be the man that eateth any food this day. And the people were faint.</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> Then said Jonathan, My father hath troubled the land: see, I pray you, how mine eyes have been enlightened, because I tasted a little of this honey.</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> How much more, if haply the people had eaten freely to day of the spoil of their enemies which they found? for had there not been now a much greater slaughter among the Philistines?</p>

<p id="v31" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">31</span> And they smote the Philistines that day from Michmash to Aijalon: and the people were very faint.</p>

<p id="v32" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">32</span> And the people flew upon the spoil, and took sheep, and oxen, and calves, and slew them on the ground: and the people did eat them with the blood.</p>

<p id="v33" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">33</span> Then they told Saul, saying, Behold, the people sin against the LORD, in that they eat with the blood. And he said, Ye have transgressed: roll a great stone unto me this day.</p>

<p id="v34" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">34</span> And Saul said, Disperse yourselves among the people, and say unto them, Bring me hither every man his ox, and every man his sheep, and slay them here, and eat; and sin not against the LORD in eating with the blood. And all the people brought every man his ox with him that night, and slew them there.</p>

<p id="v35" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">35</span> And Saul built an altar unto the LORD: the same was the first altar that he built unto the LORD.</p>

<p id="v36" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">36</span> And Saul said, Let us go down after the Philistines by night, and spoil them until the morning light, and let us not leave a man of them. And they said, Do whatsoever seemeth good unto thee. Then said the priest, Let us draw near hither unto God.</p>

<p id="v37" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">37</span> And Saul asked counsel of God, Shall I go down after the Philistines? wilt thou deliver them into the hand of Israel? But he answered him not that day.</p>

<p id="v38" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">38</span> And Saul said, Draw ye near hither, all the chief of the people: and know and see wherein this sin hath been this day.</p>

<p id="v39" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">39</span> For, as the LORD liveth, which saveth Israel, though it be in Jonathan my son, he shall surely die. But there was not a man among all the people that answered him.</p>

<p id="v40" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">40</span> Then said he unto all Israel, Be ye on one side, and I and Jonathan my son will be on the other side. And the people said unto Saul, Do what seemeth good unto thee.</p>

<p id="v41" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">41</span> Therefore Saul said unto the LORD God of Israel, Give a perfect lot. And Saul and Jonathan were taken: but the people escaped.</p>

<p id="v42" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">42</span> And Saul said, Cast lots between me and Jonathan my son. And Jonathan was taken.</p>

<p id="v43" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">43</span> Then Saul said to Jonathan, Tell me what thou hast done. And Jonathan told him, and said, I did but taste a little honey with the end of the rod that was in mine hand, and, lo, I must die.</p>

<p id="v44" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">44</span> And Saul answered, God do so and more also: for thou shalt surely die, Jonathan.</p>

<p id="v45" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">45</span> And the people said unto Saul, Shall Jonathan die, who hath wrought this great salvation in Israel? God forbid: as the LORD liveth, there shall not one hair of his head fall to the ground; for he hath wrought with God this day. So the people rescued Jonathan, that he died not.</p>

<p id="v46" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">46</span> Then Saul went up from following the Philistines: and the Philistines went to their own place.</p>

<p id="v47" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">47</span> So Saul took the kingdom over Israel, and fought against all his enemies on every side, against Moab, and against the children of Ammon, and against Edom, and against the kings of Zobah, and against the Philistines: and whithersoever he turned himself, he vexed them.</p>

<p id="v48" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">48</span> And he gathered an host, and smote the Amalekites, and delivered Israel out of the hands of them that spoiled them.</p>

<p id="v49" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">49</span> Now the sons of Saul were Jonathan, and Ishui, and Melchi-shua: and the names of his two daughters were these; the name of the firstborn Merab, and the name of the younger Michal:</p>

<p id="v50" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">50</span> And the name of Saul's wife was Ahinoam, the daughter of Ahimaaz: and the name of the captain of his host was Abner, the son of Ner, Saul's uncle.</p>

<p id="v51" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">51</span> And Kish was the father of Saul; and Ner the father of Abner was the son of Abiel.</p>

<p id="v52" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">52</span> And there was sore war against the Philistines all the days of Saul: and when Saul saw any strong man, or any valiant man, he took him unto him.</p>
