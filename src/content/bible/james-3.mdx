## James 3

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> My brethren, be not many masters, knowing that we shall receive the greater condemnation.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> For in many things we offend all. If any man offend not in word, the same is a perfect man, and able also to bridle the whole body.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> Behold, we put bits in the horses' mouths, that they may obey us; and we turn about their whole body.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Behold also the ships, which though they be so great, and are driven of fierce winds, yet are they turned about with a very small helm, whithersoever the governor listeth.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Even so the tongue is a little member, and boasteth great things. Behold, how great a matter a little fire kindleth!</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> And the tongue is a fire, a world of iniquity: so is the tongue among our members, that it defileth the whole body, and setteth on fire the course of nature; and it is set on fire of hell.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> For every kind of beasts, and of birds, and of serpents, and of things in the sea, is tamed, and hath been tamed of mankind:</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> But the tongue can no man tame; it is an unruly evil, full of deadly poison.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Therewith bless we God, even the Father; and therewith curse we men, which are made after the similitude of God.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Out of the same mouth proceedeth blessing and cursing. My brethren, these things ought not so to be.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Doth a fountain send forth at the same place sweet water and bitter?</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> Can the fig tree, my brethren, bear olive berries? either a vine, figs? so can no fountain both yield salt water and fresh.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Who is a wise man and endued with knowledge among you? let him shew out of a good conversation his works with meekness of wisdom.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> But if ye have bitter envying and strife in your hearts, glory not, and lie not against the truth.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> This wisdom descendeth not from above, but is earthly, sensual, devilish.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> For where envying and strife is, there is confusion and every evil work.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> But the wisdom that is from above is first pure, then peaceable, gentle, and easy to be intreated, full of mercy and good fruits, without partiality, and without hypocrisy.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> And the fruit of righteousness is sown in peace of them that make peace.</p>
