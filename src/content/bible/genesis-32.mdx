## Genesis 32

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> And Jacob went on his way, and the angels of God met him.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> And when Jacob saw them, he said, This is God's host: and he called the name of that place Mahanaim.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> And Jacob sent messengers before him to Esau his brother unto the land of Seir, the country of Edom.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> And he commanded them, saying, Thus shall ye speak unto my lord Esau; Thy servant Jacob saith thus, I have sojourned with Laban, and stayed there until now:</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> And I have oxen, and asses, flocks, and menservants, and womenservants: and I have sent to tell my lord, that I may find grace in thy sight.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> And the messengers returned to Jacob, saying, We came to thy brother Esau, and also he cometh to meet thee, and four hundred men with him.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> Then Jacob was greatly afraid and distressed: and he divided the people that was with him, and the flocks, and herds, and the camels, into two bands;</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> And said, If Esau come to the one company, and smite it, then the other company which is left shall escape.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> And Jacob said, O God of my father Abraham, and God of my father Isaac, the LORD which saidst unto me, Return unto thy country, and to thy kindred, and I will deal well with thee:</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> I am not worthy of the least of all the mercies, and of all the truth, which thou hast shewed unto thy servant; for with my staff I passed over this Jordan; and now I am become two bands.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Deliver me, I pray thee, from the hand of my brother, from the hand of Esau: for I fear him, lest he will come and smite me, and the mother with the children.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> And thou saidst, I will surely do thee good, and make thy seed as the sand of the sea, which cannot be numbered for multitude.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> And he lodged there that same night; and took of that which came to his hand a present for Esau his brother;</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> Two hundred she goats, and twenty he goats, two hundred ewes, and twenty rams,</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> Thirty milch camels with their colts, forty kine, and ten bulls, twenty she asses, and ten foals.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> And he delivered them into the hand of his servants, every drove by themselves; and said unto his servants, Pass over before me, and put a space betwixt drove and drove.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> And he commanded the foremost, saying, When Esau my brother meeteth thee, and asketh thee, saying, Whose art thou? and whither goest thou? and whose are these before thee?</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> Then thou shalt say, They be thy servant Jacob's; it is a present sent unto my lord Esau: and, behold, also he is behind us.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> And so commanded he the second, and the third, and all that followed the droves, saying, On this manner shall ye speak unto Esau, when ye find him.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> And say ye moreover, Behold, thy servant Jacob is behind us. For he said, I will appease him with the present that goeth before me, and afterward I will see his face; peradventure he will accept of me.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> So went the present over before him: and himself lodged that night in the company.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> And he rose up that night, and took his two wives, and his two womenservants, and his eleven sons, and passed over the ford Jabbok.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> And he took them, and sent them over the brook, and sent over that he had.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> And Jacob was left alone; and there wrestled a man with him until the breaking of the day.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> And when he saw that he prevailed not against him, he touched the hollow of his thigh; and the hollow of Jacob's thigh was out of joint, as he wrestled with him.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> And he said, Let me go, for the day breaketh. And he said, I will not let thee go, except thou bless me.</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> And he said unto him, What is thy name? And he said, Jacob.</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> And he said, Thy name shall be called no more Jacob, but Israel: for as a prince hast thou power with God and with men, and hast prevailed.</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> And Jacob asked him, and said, Tell me, I pray thee, thy name. And he said, Wherefore is it that thou dost ask after my name? And he blessed him there.</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> And Jacob called the name of the place Peniel: for I have seen God face to face, and my life is preserved.</p>

<p id="v31" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">31</span> And as he passed over Penuel the sun rose upon him, and he halted upon his thigh.</p>

<p id="v32" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">32</span> Therefore the children of Israel eat not of the sinew which shrank, which is upon the hollow of the thigh, unto this day: because he touched the hollow of Jacob's thigh in the sinew that shrank.</p>
