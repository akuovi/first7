## Genesis 25

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Then again Abraham took a wife, and her name was Keturah.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> And she bare him Zimran, and Jokshan, and Medan, and Midian, and Ishbak, and Shuah.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> And Jokshan begat Sheba, and Dedan. And the sons of Dedan were Asshurim, and Letushim, and Leummim.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> And the sons of Midian; Ephah, and Epher, and Hanoch, and Abida, and Eldaah. All these were the children of Keturah.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> And Abraham gave all that he had unto Isaac.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> But unto the sons of the concubines, which Abraham had, Abraham gave gifts, and sent them away from Isaac his son, while he yet lived, eastward, unto the east country.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> And these are the days of the years of Abraham's life which he lived, an hundred threescore and fifteen years.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Then Abraham gave up the ghost, and died in a good old age, an old man, and full of years; and was gathered to his people.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> And his sons Isaac and Ishmael buried him in the cave of Machpelah, in the field of Ephron the son of Zohar the Hittite, which is before Mamre;</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> The field which Abraham purchased of the sons of Heth: there was Abraham buried, and Sarah his wife.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> And it came to pass after the death of Abraham, that God blessed his son Isaac; and Isaac dwelt by the well Lahai-roi.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> Now these are the generations of Ishmael, Abraham's son, whom Hagar the Egyptian, Sarah's handmaid, bare unto Abraham:</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> And these are the names of the sons of Ishmael, by their names, according to their generations: the firstborn of Ishmael, Nebajoth; and Kedar, and Adbeel, and Mibsam,</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> And Mishma, and Dumah, and Massa,</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> Hadar, and Tema, Jetur, Naphish, and Kedemah:</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> These are the sons of Ishmael, and these are their names, by their towns, and by their castles; twelve princes according to their nations.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> And these are the years of the life of Ishmael, an hundred and thirty and seven years: and he gave up the ghost and died; and was gathered unto his people.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> And they dwelt from Havilah unto Shur, that is before Egypt, as thou goest toward Assyria: and he died in the presence of all his brethren.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> And these are the generations of Isaac, Abraham's son: Abraham begat Isaac:</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> And Isaac was forty years old when he took Rebekah to wife, the daughter of Bethuel the Syrian of Padan-aram, the sister to Laban the Syrian.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> And Isaac intreated the LORD for his wife, because she was barren: and the LORD was intreated of him, and Rebekah his wife conceived.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> And the children struggled together within her; and she said, If it be so, why am I thus? And she went to enquire of the LORD.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> And the LORD said unto her, Two nations are in thy womb, and two manner of people shall be separated from thy bowels; and the one people shall be stronger than the other people; and the elder shall serve the younger.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> And when her days to be delivered were fulfilled, behold, there were twins in her womb.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> And the first came out red, all over like an hairy garment; and they called his name Esau.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> And after that came his brother out, and his hand took hold on Esau's heel; and his name was called Jacob: and Isaac was threescore years old when she bare them.</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> And the boys grew: and Esau was a cunning hunter, a man of the field; and Jacob was a plain man, dwelling in tents.</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> And Isaac loved Esau, because he did eat of his venison: but Rebekah loved Jacob.</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> And Jacob sod pottage: and Esau came from the field, and he was faint:</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> And Esau said to Jacob, Feed me, I pray thee, with that same red pottage; for I am faint: therefore was his name called Edom.</p>

<p id="v31" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">31</span> And Jacob said, Sell me this day thy birthright.</p>

<p id="v32" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">32</span> And Esau said, Behold, I am at the point to die: and what profit shall this birthright do to me?</p>

<p id="v33" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">33</span> And Jacob said, Swear to me this day; and he sware unto him: and he sold his birthright unto Jacob.</p>

<p id="v34" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">34</span> Then Jacob gave Esau bread and pottage of lentiles; and he did eat and drink, and rose up, and went his way: thus Esau despised his birthright.</p>
