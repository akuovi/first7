## Job 32

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> So these three men ceased to answer Job, because he was righteous in his own eyes.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> Then was kindled the wrath of Elihu the son of Barachel the Buzite, of the kindred of Ram: against Job was his wrath kindled, because he justified himself rather than God.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> Also against his three friends was his wrath kindled, because they had found no answer, and yet had condemned Job.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Now Elihu had waited till Job had spoken, because they were elder than he.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> When Elihu saw that there was no answer in the mouth of these three men, then his wrath was kindled.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> And Elihu the son of Barachel the Buzite answered and said, I am young, and ye are very old; wherefore I was afraid, and durst not shew you mine opinion.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> I said, Days should speak, and multitude of years should teach wisdom.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> But there is a spirit in man: and the inspiration of the Almighty giveth them understanding.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Great men are not always wise: neither do the aged understand judgment.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Therefore I said, Hearken to me; I also will shew mine opinion.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Behold, I waited for your words; I gave ear to your reasons, whilst ye searched out what to say.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> Yea, I attended unto you, and, behold, there was none of you that convinced Job, or that answered his words:</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Lest ye should say, We have found out wisdom: God thrusteth him down, not man.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> Now he hath not directed his words against me: neither will I answer him with your speeches.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> They were amazed, they answered no more: they left off speaking.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> When I had waited, (for they spake not, but stood still, and answered no more;)</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> I said, I will answer also my part, I also will shew mine opinion.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> For I am full of matter, the spirit within me constraineth me.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> Behold, my belly is as wine which hath no vent; it is ready to burst like new bottles.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> I will speak, that I may be refreshed: I will open my lips and answer.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> Let me not, I pray you, accept any man's person, neither let me give flattering titles unto man.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> For I know not to give flattering titles; in so doing my maker would soon take me away.</p>
