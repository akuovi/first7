## Ecclesiastes 10

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Dead flies cause the ointment of the apothecary to send forth a stinking savour: so doth a little folly him that is in reputation for wisdom and honour.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> A wise man's heart is at his right hand; but a fool's heart at his left.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> Yea also, when he that is a fool walketh by the way, his wisdom faileth him, and he saith to every one that he is a fool.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> If the spirit of the ruler rise up against thee, leave not thy place; for yielding pacifieth great offences.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> There is an evil which I have seen under the sun, as an error which proceedeth from the ruler:</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> Folly is set in great dignity, and the rich sit in low place.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> I have seen servants upon horses, and princes walking as servants upon the earth.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> He that diggeth a pit shall fall into it; and whoso breaketh an hedge, a serpent shall bite him.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Whoso removeth stones shall be hurt therewith; and he that cleaveth wood shall be endangered thereby.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> If the iron be blunt, and he do not whet the edge, then must he put to more strength: but wisdom is profitable to direct.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Surely the serpent will bite without enchantment; and a babbler is no better.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> The words of a wise man's mouth are gracious; but the lips of a fool will swallow up himself.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> The beginning of the words of his mouth is foolishness: and the end of his talk is mischievous madness.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> A fool also is full of words: a man cannot tell what shall be; and what shall be after him, who can tell him?</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> The labour of the foolish wearieth every one of them, because he knoweth not how to go to the city.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> Woe to thee, O land, when thy king is a child, and thy princes eat in the morning!</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> Blessed art thou, O land, when thy king is the son of nobles, and thy princes eat in due season, for strength, and not for drunkenness!</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> By much slothfulness the building decayeth; and through idleness of the hands the house droppeth through.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> A feast is made for laughter, and wine maketh merry: but money answereth all things.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> Curse not the king, no not in thy thought; and curse not the rich in thy bedchamber: for a bird of the air shall carry the voice, and that which hath wings shall tell the matter.</p>
