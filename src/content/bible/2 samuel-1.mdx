## 2 Samuel 1

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Now it came to pass after the death of Saul, when David was returned from the slaughter of the Amalekites, and David had abode two days in Ziklag;</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> It came even to pass on the third day, that, behold, a man came out of the camp from Saul with his clothes rent, and earth upon his head: and so it was, when he came to David, that he fell to the earth, and did obeisance.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> And David said unto him, From whence comest thou? And he said unto him, Out of the camp of Israel am I escaped.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> And David said unto him, How went the matter? I pray thee, tell me. And he answered, That the people are fled from the battle, and many of the people also are fallen and dead; and Saul and Jonathan his son are dead also.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> And David said unto the young man that told him, How knowest thou that Saul and Jonathan his son be dead?</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> And the young man that told him said, As I happened by chance upon mount Gilboa, behold, Saul leaned upon his spear; and, lo, the chariots and horsemen followed hard after him.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> And when he looked behind him, he saw me, and called unto me. And I answered, Here am I.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> And he said unto me, Who art thou? And I answered him, I am an Amalekite.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> He said unto me again, Stand, I pray thee, upon me, and slay me: for anguish is come upon me, because my life is yet whole in me.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> So I stood upon him, and slew him, because I was sure that he could not live after that he was fallen: and I took the crown that was upon his head, and the bracelet that was on his arm, and have brought them hither unto my lord.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Then David took hold on his clothes, and rent them; and likewise all the men that were with him:</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> And they mourned, and wept, and fasted until even, for Saul, and for Jonathan his son, and for the people of the LORD, and for the house of Israel; because they were fallen by the sword.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> And David said unto the young man that told him, Whence art thou? And he answered, I am the son of a stranger, an Amalekite.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> And David said unto him, How wast thou not afraid to stretch forth thine hand to destroy the LORD'S anointed?</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> And David called one of the young men, and said, Go near, and fall upon him. And he smote him that he died.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> And David said unto him, Thy blood be upon thy head; for thy mouth hath testified against thee, saying, I have slain the LORD'S anointed.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> And David lamented with this lamentation over Saul and over Jonathan his son:</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> (Also he bade them teach the children of Judah the use of the bow: behold, it is written in the book of Jasher.)</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> The beauty of Israel is slain upon thy high places: how are the mighty fallen!</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> Tell it not in Gath, publish it not in the streets of Askelon; lest the daughters of the Philistines rejoice, lest the daughters of the uncircumcised triumph.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> Ye mountains of Gilboa, let there be no dew, neither let there be rain, upon you, nor fields of offerings: for there the shield of the mighty is vilely cast away, the shield of Saul, as though he had not been anointed with oil.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> From the blood of the slain, from the fat of the mighty, the bow of Jonathan turned not back, and the sword of Saul returned not empty.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> Saul and Jonathan were lovely and pleasant in their lives, and in their death they were not divided: they were swifter than eagles, they were stronger than lions.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> Ye daughters of Israel, weep over Saul, who clothed you in scarlet, with other delights, who put on ornaments of gold upon your apparel.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> How are the mighty fallen in the midst of the battle! O Jonathan, thou wast slain in thine high places.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> I am distressed for thee, my brother Jonathan: very pleasant hast thou been unto me: thy love to me was wonderful, passing the love of women.</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> How are the mighty fallen, and the weapons of war perished!</p>
