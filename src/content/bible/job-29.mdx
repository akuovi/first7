## Job 29

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Moreover Job continued his parable, and said,</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> Oh that I were as in months past, as in the days when God preserved me;</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> When his candle shined upon my head, and when by his light I walked through darkness;</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> As I was in the days of my youth, when the secret of God was upon my tabernacle;</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> When the Almighty was yet with me, when my children were about me;</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> When I washed my steps with butter, and the rock poured me out rivers of oil;</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> When I went out to the gate through the city, when I prepared my seat in the street!</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> The young men saw me, and hid themselves: and the aged arose, and stood up.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> The princes refrained talking, and laid their hand on their mouth.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> The nobles held their peace, and their tongue cleaved to the roof of their mouth.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> When the ear heard me, then it blessed me; and when the eye saw me, it gave witness to me:</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> Because I delivered the poor that cried, and the fatherless, and him that had none to help him.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> The blessing of him that was ready to perish came upon me: and I caused the widow's heart to sing for joy.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> I put on righteousness, and it clothed me: my judgment was as a robe and a diadem.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> I was eyes to the blind, and feet was I to the lame.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> I was a father to the poor: and the cause which I knew not I searched out.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> And I brake the jaws of the wicked, and plucked the spoil out of his teeth.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> Then I said, I shall die in my nest, and I shall multiply my days as the sand.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> My root was spread out by the waters, and the dew lay all night upon my branch.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> My glory was fresh in me, and my bow was renewed in my hand.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> Unto me men gave ear, and waited, and kept silence at my counsel.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> After my words they spake not again; and my speech dropped upon them.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> And they waited for me as for the rain; and they opened their mouth wide as for the latter rain.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> If I laughed on them, they believed it not; and the light of my countenance they cast not down.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> I chose out their way, and sat chief, and dwelt as a king in the army, as one that comforteth the mourners.</p>
