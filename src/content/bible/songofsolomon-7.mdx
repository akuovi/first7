## SongofSolomon 7

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> How beautiful are thy feet with shoes, O prince's daughter! the joints of thy thighs are like jewels, the work of the hands of a cunning workman.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> Thy navel is like a round goblet, which wanteth not liquor: thy belly is like an heap of wheat set about with lilies.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> Thy two breasts are like two young roes that are twins.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Thy neck is as a tower of ivory; thine eyes like the fishpools in Heshbon, by the gate of Bath-rabbim: thy nose is as the tower of Lebanon which looketh toward Damascus.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Thine head upon thee is like Carmel, and the hair of thine head like purple; the king is held in the galleries.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> How fair and how pleasant art thou, O love, for delights!</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> This thy stature is like to a palm tree, and thy breasts to clusters of grapes.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> I said, I will go up to the palm tree, I will take hold of the boughs thereof: now also thy breasts shall be as clusters of the vine, and the smell of thy nose like apples;</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> And the roof of thy mouth like the best wine for my beloved, that goeth down sweetly, causing the lips of those that are asleep to speak.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> I am my beloved's, and his desire is toward me.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Come, my beloved, let us go forth into the field; let us lodge in the villages.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> Let us get up early to the vineyards; let us see if the vine flourish, whether the tender grape appear, and the pomegranates bud forth: there will I give thee my loves.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> The mandrakes give a smell, and at our gates are all manner of pleasant fruits, new and old, which I have laid up for thee, O my beloved.</p>
