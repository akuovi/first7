## Jonah 4

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> But it displeased Jonah exceedingly, and he was very angry.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> And he prayed unto the LORD, and said, I pray thee, O LORD, was not this my saying, when I was yet in my country? Therefore I fled before unto Tarshish: for I knew that thou art a gracious God, and merciful, slow to anger, and of great kindness, and repentest thee of the evil.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> Therefore now, O LORD, take, I beseech thee, my life from me; for it is better for me to die than to live.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Then said the LORD, Doest thou well to be angry?</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> So Jonah went out of the city, and sat on the east side of the city, and there made him a booth, and sat under it in the shadow, till he might see what would become of the city.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> And the LORD God prepared a gourd, and made it to come up over Jonah, that it might be a shadow over his head, to deliver him from his grief. So Jonah was exceeding glad of the gourd.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> But God prepared a worm when the morning rose the next day, and it smote the gourd that it withered.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> And it came to pass, when the sun did arise, that God prepared a vehement east wind; and the sun beat upon the head of Jonah, that he fainted, and wished in himself to die, and said, It is better for me to die than to live.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> And God said to Jonah, Doest thou well to be angry for the gourd? And he said, I do well to be angry, even unto death.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Then said the LORD, Thou hast had pity on the gourd, for the which thou hast not laboured, neither madest it grow; which came up in a night, and perished in a night:</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> And should not I spare Nineveh, that great city, wherein are more than sixscore thousand persons that cannot discern between their right hand and their left hand; and also much cattle?</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> JONAH</p>
