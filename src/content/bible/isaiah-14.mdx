## Isaiah 14

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> For the LORD will have mercy on Jacob, and will yet choose Israel, and set them in their own land: and the strangers shall be joined with them, and they shall cleave to the house of Jacob.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> And the people shall take them, and bring them to their place: and the house of Israel shall possess them in the land of the LORD for servants and handmaids: and they shall take them captives, whose captives they were; and they shall rule over their oppressors.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> And it shall come to pass in the day that the LORD shall give thee rest from thy sorrow, and from thy fear, and from the hard bondage wherein thou wast made to serve,</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> That thou shalt take up this proverb against the king of Babylon, and say, How hath the oppressor ceased! the golden city ceased!</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> The LORD hath broken the staff of the wicked, and the sceptre of the rulers.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> He who smote the people in wrath with a continual stroke, he that ruled the nations in anger, is persecuted, and none hindereth.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> The whole earth is at rest, and is quiet: they break forth into singing.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Yea, the fir trees rejoice at thee, and the cedars of Lebanon, saying, Since thou art laid down, no feller is come up against us.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Hell from beneath is moved for thee to meet thee at thy coming: it stirreth up the dead for thee, even all the chief ones of the earth; it hath raised up from their thrones all the kings of the nations.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> All they shall speak and say unto thee, Art thou also become weak as we? art thou become like unto us?</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Thy pomp is brought down to the grave, and the noise of thy viols: the worm is spread under thee, and the worms cover thee.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> How art thou fallen from heaven, O Lucifer, son of the morning! how art thou cut down to the ground, which didst weaken the nations!</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> For thou hast said in thine heart, I will ascend into heaven, I will exalt my throne above the stars of God: I will sit also upon the mount of the congregation, in the sides of the north:</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> I will ascend above the heights of the clouds; I will be like the most High.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> Yet thou shalt be brought down to hell, to the sides of the pit.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> They that see thee shall narrowly look upon thee, and consider thee, saying, Is this the man that made the earth to tremble, that did shake kingdoms;</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> That made the world as a wilderness, and destroyed the cities thereof; that opened not the house of his prisoners?</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> All the kings of the nations, even all of them, lie in glory, every one in his own house.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> But thou art cast out of thy grave like an abominable branch, and as the raiment of those that are slain, thrust through with a sword, that go down to the stones of the pit; as a carcase trodden under feet.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> Thou shalt not be joined with them in burial, because thou hast destroyed thy land, and slain thy people: the seed of evildoers shall never be renowned.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> Prepare slaughter for his children for the iniquity of their fathers; that they do not rise, nor possess the land, nor fill the face of the world with cities.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> For I will rise up against them, saith the LORD of hosts, and cut off from Babylon the name, and remnant, and son, and nephew, saith the LORD.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> I will also make it a possession for the bittern, and pools of water: and I will sweep it with the besom of destruction, saith the LORD of hosts.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> The LORD of hosts hath sworn, saying, Surely as I have thought, so shall it come to pass; and as I have purposed, so shall it stand:</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> That I will break the Assyrian in my land, and upon my mountains tread him under foot: then shall his yoke depart from off them, and his burden depart from off their shoulders.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> This is the purpose that is purposed upon the whole earth: and this is the hand that is stretched out upon all the nations.</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> For the LORD of hosts hath purposed, and who shall disannul it? and his hand is stretched out, and who shall turn it back?</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> In the year that king Ahaz died was this burden.</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> Rejoice not thou, whole Palestina, because the rod of him that smote thee is broken: for out of the serpent's root shall come forth a cockatrice, and his fruit shall be a fiery flying serpent.</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> And the firstborn of the poor shall feed, and the needy shall lie down in safety: and I will kill thy root with famine, and he shall slay thy remnant.</p>

<p id="v31" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">31</span> Howl, O gate; cry, O city; thou, whole Palestina, art dissolved: for there shall come from the north a smoke, and none shall be alone in his appointed times.</p>

<p id="v32" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">32</span> What shall one then answer the messengers of the nation? That the LORD hath founded Zion, and the poor of his people shall trust in it.</p>
