## Luke 6

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> And it came to pass on the second sabbath after the first, that he went through the corn fields; and his disciples plucked the ears of corn, and did eat, rubbing them in their hands.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> And certain of the Pharisees said unto them, Why do ye that which is not lawful to do on the sabbath days?</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> And Jesus answering them said, Have ye not read so much as this, what David did, when himself was an hungred, and they which were with him;</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> How he went into the house of God, and did take and eat the shewbread, and gave also to them that were with him; which it is not lawful to eat but for the priests alone?</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> And he said unto them, That the Son of man is Lord also of the sabbath.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> And it came to pass also on another sabbath, that he entered into the synagogue and taught: and there was a man whose right hand was withered.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> And the scribes and Pharisees watched him, whether he would heal on the sabbath day; that they might find an accusation against him.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> But he knew their thoughts, and said to the man which had the withered hand, Rise up, and stand forth in the midst. And he arose and stood forth.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Then said Jesus unto them, I will ask you one thing; Is it lawful on the sabbath days to do good, or to do evil? to save life, or to destroy it?</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> And looking round about upon them all, he said unto the man, Stretch forth thy hand. And he did so: and his hand was restored whole as the other.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> And they were filled with madness; and communed one with another what they might do to Jesus.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> And it came to pass in those days, that he went out into a mountain to pray, and continued all night in prayer to God.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> And when it was day, he called unto him his disciples: and of them he chose twelve, whom also he named apostles;</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> Simon, (whom he also named Peter,) and Andrew his brother, James and John, Philip and Bartholomew,</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> Matthew and Thomas, James the son of Alphaeus, and Simon called Zelotes,</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> And Judas the brother of James, and Judas Iscariot, which also was the traitor.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> And he came down with them, and stood in the plain, and the company of his disciples, and a great multitude of people out of all Judaea and Jerusalem, and from the sea coast of Tyre and Sidon, which came to hear him, and to be healed of their diseases;</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> And they that were vexed with unclean spirits: and they were healed.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> And the whole multitude sought to touch him: for there went virtue out of him, and healed them all.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> And he lifted up his eyes on his disciples, and said, Blessed be ye poor: for your's is the kingdom of God.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> Blessed are ye that hunger now: for ye shall be filled. Blessed are ye that weep now: for ye shall laugh.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> Blessed are ye, when men shall hate you, and when they shall separate you from their company, and shall reproach you, and cast out your name as evil, for the Son of man's sake.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> Rejoice ye in that day, and leap for joy: for, behold, your reward is great in heaven: for in the like manner did their fathers unto the prophets.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> But woe unto you that are rich! for ye have received your consolation.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> Woe unto you that are full! for ye shall hunger. Woe unto you that laugh now! for ye shall mourn and weep.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> Woe unto you, when all men shall speak well of you! for so did their fathers to the false prophets.</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> But I say unto you which hear, Love your enemies, do good to them which hate you,</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> Bless them that curse you, and pray for them which despitefully use you.</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> And unto him that smiteth thee on the one cheek offer also the other; and him that taketh away thy cloke forbid not to take thy coat also.</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> Give to every man that asketh of thee; and of him that taketh away thy goods ask them not again.</p>

<p id="v31" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">31</span> And as ye would that men should do to you, do ye also to them likewise.</p>

<p id="v32" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">32</span> For if ye love them which love you, what thank have ye? for sinners also love those that love them.</p>

<p id="v33" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">33</span> And if ye do good to them which do good to you, what thank have ye? for sinners also do even the same.</p>

<p id="v34" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">34</span> And if ye lend to them of whom ye hope to receive, what thank have ye? for sinners also lend to sinners, to receive as much again.</p>

<p id="v35" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">35</span> But love ye your enemies, and do good, and lend, hoping for nothing again; and your reward shall be great, and ye shall be the children of the Highest: for he is kind unto the unthankful and to the evil.</p>

<p id="v36" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">36</span> Be ye therefore merciful, as your Father also is merciful.</p>

<p id="v37" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">37</span> Judge not, and ye shall not be judged: condemn not, and ye shall not be condemned: forgive, and ye shall be forgiven:</p>

<p id="v38" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">38</span> Give, and it shall be given unto you; good measure, pressed down, and shaken together, and running over, shall men give into your bosom. For with the same measure that ye mete withal it shall be measured to you again.</p>

<p id="v39" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">39</span> And he spake a parable unto them, Can the blind lead the blind? shall they not both fall into the ditch?</p>

<p id="v40" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">40</span> The disciple is not above his master: but every one that is perfect shall be as his master.</p>

<p id="v41" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">41</span> And why beholdest thou the mote that is in thy brother's eye, but perceivest not the beam that is in thine own eye?</p>

<p id="v42" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">42</span> Either how canst thou say to thy brother, Brother, let me pull out the mote that is in thine eye, when thou thyself beholdest not the beam that is in thine own eye? Thou hypocrite, cast out first the beam out of thine own eye, and then shalt thou see clearly to pull out the mote that is in thy brother's eye.</p>

<p id="v43" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">43</span> For a good tree bringeth not forth corrupt fruit; neither doth a corrupt tree bring forth good fruit.</p>

<p id="v44" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">44</span> For every tree is known by his own fruit. For of thorns men do not gather figs, nor of a bramble bush gather they grapes.</p>

<p id="v45" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">45</span> A good man out of the good treasure of his heart bringeth forth that which is good; and an evil man out of the evil treasure of his heart bringeth forth that which is evil: for of the abundance of the heart his mouth speaketh.</p>

<p id="v46" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">46</span> And why call ye me, Lord, Lord, and do not the things which I say?</p>

<p id="v47" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">47</span> Whosoever cometh to me, and heareth my sayings, and doeth them, I will shew you to whom he is like:</p>

<p id="v48" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">48</span> He is like a man which built an house, and digged deep, and laid the foundation on a rock: and when the flood arose, the stream beat vehemently upon that house, and could not shake it: for it was founded upon a rock.</p>

<p id="v49" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">49</span> But he that heareth, and doeth not, is like a man that without a foundation built an house upon the earth; against which the stream did beat vehemently, and immediately it fell; and the ruin of that house was great.</p>
