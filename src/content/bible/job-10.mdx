## Job 10

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> My soul is weary of my life; I will leave my complaint upon myself; I will speak in the bitterness of my soul.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> I will say unto God, Do not condemn me; shew me wherefore thou contendest with me.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> Is it good unto thee that thou shouldest oppress, that thou shouldest despise the work of thine hands, and shine upon the counsel of the wicked?</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Hast thou eyes of flesh? or seest thou as man seeth?</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Are thy days as the days of man? are thy years as man's days,</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> That thou enquirest after mine iniquity, and searchest after my sin?</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> Thou knowest that I am not wicked; and there is none that can deliver out of thine hand.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Thine hands have made me and fashioned me together round about; yet thou dost destroy me.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Remember, I beseech thee, that thou hast made me as the clay; and wilt thou bring me into dust again?</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Hast thou not poured me out as milk, and curdled me like cheese?</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Thou hast clothed me with skin and flesh, and hast fenced me with bones and sinews.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> Thou hast granted me life and favour, and thy visitation hath preserved my spirit.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> And these things hast thou hid in thine heart: I know that this is with thee.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> If I sin, then thou markest me, and thou wilt not acquit me from mine iniquity.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> If I be wicked, woe unto me; and if I be righteous, yet will I not lift up my head. I am full of confusion; therefore see thou mine affliction;</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> For it increaseth. Thou huntest me as a fierce lion: and again thou shewest thyself marvellous upon me.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> Thou renewest thy witnesses against me, and increasest thine indignation upon me; changes and war are against me.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> Wherefore then hast thou brought me forth out of the womb? Oh that I had given up the ghost, and no eye had seen me!</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> I should have been as though I had not been; I should have been carried from the womb to the grave.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> Are not my days few? cease then, and let me alone, that I may take comfort a little,</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> Before I go whence I shall not return, even to the land of darkness and the shadow of death;</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> A land of darkness, as darkness itself; and of the shadow of death, without any order, and where the light is as darkness.</p>
