## Micah 6

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Hear ye now what the LORD saith; Arise, contend thou before the mountains, and let the hills hear thy voice.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> Hear ye, O mountains, the LORD'S controversy, and ye strong foundations of the earth: for the LORD hath a controversy with his people, and he will plead with Israel.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> O my people, what have I done unto thee? and wherein have I wearied thee? testify against me.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> For I brought thee up out of the land of Egypt, and redeemed thee out of the house of servants; and I sent before thee Moses, Aaron, and Miriam.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> O my people, remember now what Balak king of Moab consulted, and what Balaam the son of Beor answered him from Shittim unto Gilgal; that ye may know the righteousness of the LORD.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> Wherewith shall I come before the LORD, and bow myself before the high God? shall I come before him with burnt offerings, with calves of a year old?</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> Will the LORD be pleased with thousands of rams, or with ten thousands of rivers of oil? shall I give my firstborn for my transgression, the fruit of my body for the sin of my soul?</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> He hath shewed thee, O man, what is good; and what doth the LORD require of thee, but to do justly, and to love mercy, and to walk humbly with thy God?</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> The LORD'S voice crieth unto the city, and the man of wisdom shall see thy name: hear ye the rod, and who hath appointed it.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Are there yet the treasures of wickedness in the house of the wicked, and the scant measure that is abominable?</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Shall I count them pure with the wicked balances, and with the bag of deceitful weights?</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> For the rich men thereof are full of violence, and the inhabitants thereof have spoken lies, and their tongue is deceitful in their mouth.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Therefore also will I make thee sick in smiting thee, in making thee desolate because of thy sins.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> Thou shalt eat, but not be satisfied; and thy casting down shall be in the midst of thee; and thou shalt take hold, but shalt not deliver; and that which thou deliverest will I give up to the sword.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> Thou shalt sow, but thou shalt not reap; thou shalt tread the olives, but thou shalt not anoint thee with oil; and sweet wine, but shalt not drink wine.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> For the statutes of Omri are kept, and all the works of the house of Ahab, and ye walk in their counsels; that I should make thee a desolation, and the inhabitants thereof an hissing: therefore ye shall bear the reproach of my people.</p>
