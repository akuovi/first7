## 1 Corinthians 16

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Now concerning the collection for the saints, as I have given order to the churches of Galatia, even so do ye.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> Upon the first day of the week let every one of you lay by him in store, as God hath prospered him, that there be no gatherings when I come.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> And when I come, whomsoever ye shall approve by your letters, them will I send to bring your liberality unto Jerusalem.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> And if it be meet that I go also, they shall go with me.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Now I will come unto you, when I shall pass through Macedonia: for I do pass through Macedonia.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> And it may be that I will abide, yea, and winter with you, that ye may bring me on my journey whithersoever I go.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> For I will not see you now by the way; but I trust to tarry a while with you, if the Lord permit.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> But I will tarry at Ephesus until Pentecost.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> For a great door and effectual is opened unto me, and there are many adversaries.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Now if Timotheus come, see that he may be with you without fear: for he worketh the work of the Lord, as I also do.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Let no man therefore despise him: but conduct him forth in peace, that he may come unto me: for I look for him with the brethren.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> As touching our brother Apollos, I greatly desired him to come unto you with the brethren: but his will was not at all to come at this time; but he will come when he shall have convenient time.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Watch ye, stand fast in the faith, quit you like men, be strong.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> Let all your things be done with charity.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> I beseech you, brethren, (ye know the house of Stephanas, that it is the firstfruits of Achaia, and that they have addicted themselves to the ministry of the saints,)</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> That ye submit yourselves unto such, and to every one that helpeth with us, and laboureth.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> I am glad of the coming of Stephanas and Fortunatus and Achaicus: for that which was lacking on your part they have supplied.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> For they have refreshed my spirit and your's: therefore acknowledge ye them that are such.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> The churches of Asia salute you. Aquila and Priscilla salute you much in the Lord, with the church that is in their house.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> All the brethren greet you. Greet ye one another with an holy kiss.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> The salutation of me Paul with mine own hand.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> If any man love not the Lord Jesus Christ, let him be Anathema Maranatha.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> My love be with you all in Christ Jesus. Amen.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> The first epistle to the Corinthians was written from Philippi by Stephanas, and Fortunatus, and Achaicus, and Timotheus.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> CORINTHIANS</p>
