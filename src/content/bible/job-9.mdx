## Job 9

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Then Job answered and said,</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> I know it is so of a truth: but how should man be just with God?</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> If he will contend with him, he cannot answer him one of a thousand.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> He is wise in heart, and mighty in strength: who hath hardened himself against him, and hath prospered?</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Which removeth the mountains, and they know not: which overturneth them in his anger.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> Which shaketh the earth out of her place, and the pillars thereof tremble.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> Which commandeth the sun, and it riseth not; and sealeth up the stars.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Which alone spreadeth out the heavens, and treadeth upon the waves of the sea.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Which maketh Arcturus, Orion, and Pleiades, and the chambers of the south.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Which doeth great things past finding out; yea, and wonders without number.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Lo, he goeth by me, and I see him not: he passeth on also, but I perceive him not.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> Behold, he taketh away, who can hinder him? who will say unto him, What doest thou?</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> If God will not withdraw his anger, the proud helpers do stoop under him.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> How much less shall I answer him, and choose out my words to reason with him?</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> Whom, though I were righteous, yet would I not answer, but I would make supplication to my judge.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> If I had called, and he had answered me; yet would I not believe that he had hearkened unto my voice.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> For he breaketh me with a tempest, and multiplieth my wounds without cause.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> He will not suffer me to take my breath, but filleth me with bitterness.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> If I speak of strength, lo, he is strong: and if of judgment, who shall set me a time to plead?</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> If I justify myself, mine own mouth shall condemn me: if I say, I am perfect, it shall also prove me perverse.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> Though I were perfect, yet would I not know my soul: I would despise my life.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> This is one thing, therefore I said it, He destroyeth the perfect and the wicked.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> If the scourge slay suddenly, he will laugh at the trial of the innocent.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> The earth is given into the hand of the wicked: he covereth the faces of the judges thereof; if not, where, and who is he?</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> Now my days are swifter than a post: they flee away, they see no good.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> They are passed away as the swift ships: as the eagle that hasteth to the prey.</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> If I say, I will forget my complaint, I will leave off my heaviness, and comfort myself:</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> I am afraid of all my sorrows, I know that thou wilt not hold me innocent.</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> If I be wicked, why then labour I in vain?</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> If I wash myself with snow water, and make my hands never so clean;</p>

<p id="v31" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">31</span> Yet shalt thou plunge me in the ditch, and mine own clothes shall abhor me.</p>

<p id="v32" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">32</span> For he is not a man, as I am, that I should answer him, and we should come together in judgment.</p>

<p id="v33" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">33</span> Neither is there any daysman betwixt us, that might lay his hand upon us both.</p>

<p id="v34" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">34</span> Let him take his rod away from me, and let not his fear terrify me:</p>

<p id="v35" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">35</span> Then would I speak, and not fear him; but it is not so with me.</p>
