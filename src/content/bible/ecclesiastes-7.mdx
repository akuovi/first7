## Ecclesiastes 7

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> A good name is better than precious ointment; and the day of death than the day of one's birth.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> It is better to go to the house of mourning, than to go to the house of feasting: for that is the end of all men; and the living will lay it to his heart.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> Sorrow is better than laughter: for by the sadness of the countenance the heart is made better.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> The heart of the wise is in the house of mourning; but the heart of fools is in the house of mirth.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> It is better to hear the rebuke of the wise, than for a man to hear the song of fools.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> For as the crackling of thorns under a pot, so is the laughter of the fool: this also is vanity.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> Surely oppression maketh a wise man mad; and a gift destroyeth the heart.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Better is the end of a thing than the beginning thereof: and the patient in spirit is better than the proud in spirit.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Be not hasty in thy spirit to be angry: for anger resteth in the bosom of fools.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Say not thou, What is the cause that the former days were better than these? for thou dost not enquire wisely concerning this.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Wisdom is good with an inheritance: and by it there is profit to them that see the sun.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> For wisdom is a defence, and money is a defence: but the excellency of knowledge is, that wisdom giveth life to them that have it.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Consider the work of God: for who can make that straight, which he hath made crooked?</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> In the day of prosperity be joyful, but in the day of adversity consider: God also hath set the one over against the other, to the end that man should find nothing after him.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> All things have I seen in the days of my vanity: there is a just man that perisheth in his righteousness, and there is a wicked man that prolongeth his life in his wickedness.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> Be not righteous over much; neither make thyself over wise: why shouldest thou destroy thyself?</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> Be not over much wicked, neither be thou foolish: why shouldest thou die before thy time?</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> It is good that thou shouldest take hold of this; yea, also from this withdraw not thine hand: for he that feareth God shall come forth of them all.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> Wisdom strengtheneth the wise more than ten mighty men which are in the city.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> For there is not a just man upon earth, that doeth good, and sinneth not.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> Also take no heed unto all words that are spoken; lest thou hear thy servant curse thee:</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> For oftentimes also thine own heart knoweth that thou thyself likewise hast cursed others.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> All this have I proved by wisdom: I said, I will be wise; but it was far from me.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> That which is far off, and exceeding deep, who can find it out?</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> I applied mine heart to know, and to search, and to seek out wisdom, and the reason of things, and to know the wickedness of folly, even of foolishness and madness:</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> And I find more bitter than death the woman, whose heart is snares and nets, and her hands as bands: whoso pleaseth God shall escape from her; but the sinner shall be taken by her.</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> Behold, this have I found, saith the preacher, counting one by one, to find out the account:</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> Which yet my soul seeketh, but I find not: one man among a thousand have I found; but a woman among all those have I not found.</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> Lo, this only have I found, that God hath made man upright; but they have sought out many inventions.</p>
