## Job 41

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Canst thou draw out leviathan with an hook? or his tongue with a cord which thou lettest down?</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> Canst thou put an hook into his nose? or bore his jaw through with a thorn?</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> Will he make many supplications unto thee? will he speak soft words unto thee?</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Will he make a covenant with thee? wilt thou take him for a servant for ever?</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Wilt thou play with him as with a bird? or wilt thou bind him for thy maidens?</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> Shall the companions make a banquet of him? shall they part him among the merchants?</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> Canst thou fill his skin with barbed irons? or his head with fish spears?</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Lay thine hand upon him, remember the battle, do no more.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Behold, the hope of him is in vain: shall not one be cast down even at the sight of him?</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> None is so fierce that dare stir him up: who then is able to stand before me?</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Who hath prevented me, that I should repay him? whatsoever is under the whole heaven is mine.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> I will not conceal his parts, nor his power, nor his comely proportion.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Who can discover the face of his garment? or who can come to him with his double bridle?</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> Who can open the doors of his face? his teeth are terrible round about.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> His scales are his pride, shut up together as with a close seal.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> One is so near to another, that no air can come between them.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> They are joined one to another, they stick together, that they cannot be sundered.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> By his neesings a light doth shine, and his eyes are like the eyelids of the morning.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> Out of his mouth go burning lamps, and sparks of fire leap out.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> Out of his nostrils goeth smoke, as out of a seething pot or caldron.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> His breath kindleth coals, and a flame goeth out of his mouth.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> In his neck remaineth strength, and sorrow is turned into joy before him.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> The flakes of his flesh are joined together: they are firm in themselves; they cannot be moved.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> His heart is as firm as a stone; yea, as hard as a piece of the nether millstone.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> When he raiseth up himself, the mighty are afraid: by reason of breakings they purify themselves.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> The sword of him that layeth at him cannot hold: the spear, the dart, nor the habergeon.</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> He esteemeth iron as straw, and brass as rotten wood.</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> The arrow cannot make him flee: slingstones are turned with him into stubble.</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> Darts are counted as stubble: he laugheth at the shaking of a spear.</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> Sharp stones are under him: he spreadeth sharp pointed things upon the mire.</p>

<p id="v31" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">31</span> He maketh the deep to boil like a pot: he maketh the sea like a pot of ointment.</p>

<p id="v32" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">32</span> He maketh a path to shine after him; one would think the deep to be hoary.</p>

<p id="v33" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">33</span> Upon earth there is not his like, who is made without fear.</p>

<p id="v34" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">34</span> He beholdeth all high things: he is a king over all the children of pride.</p>
