## Job 13

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Lo, mine eye hath seen all this, mine ear hath heard and understood it.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> What ye know, the same do I know also: I am not inferior unto you.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> Surely I would speak to the Almighty, and I desire to reason with God.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> But ye are forgers of lies, ye are all physicians of no value.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> O that ye would altogether hold your peace! and it should be your wisdom.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> Hear now my reasoning, and hearken to the pleadings of my lips.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> Will ye speak wickedly for God? and talk deceitfully for him?</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Will ye accept his person? will ye contend for God?</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Is it good that he should search you out? or as one man mocketh another, do ye so mock him?</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> He will surely reprove you, if ye do secretly accept persons.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Shall not his excellency make you afraid? and his dread fall upon you?</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> Your remembrances are like unto ashes, your bodies to bodies of clay.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Hold your peace, let me alone, that I may speak, and let come on me what will.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> Wherefore do I take my flesh in my teeth, and put my life in mine hand?</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> Though he slay me, yet will I trust in him: but I will maintain mine own ways before him.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> He also shall be my salvation: for an hypocrite shall not come before him.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> Hear diligently my speech, and my declaration with your ears.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> Behold now, I have ordered my cause; I know that I shall be justified.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> Who is he that will plead with me? for now, if I hold my tongue, I shall give up the ghost.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> Only do not two things unto me: then will I not hide myself from thee.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> Withdraw thine hand far from me: and let not thy dread make me afraid.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> Then call thou, and I will answer: or let me speak, and answer thou me.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> How many are mine iniquities and sins? make me to know my transgression and my sin.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> Wherefore hidest thou thy face, and holdest me for thine enemy?</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> Wilt thou break a leaf driven to and fro? and wilt thou pursue the dry stubble?</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> For thou writest bitter things against me, and makest me to possess the iniquities of my youth.</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> Thou puttest my feet also in the stocks, and lookest narrowly unto all my paths; thou settest a print upon the heels of my feet.</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> And he, as a rotten thing, consumeth, as a garment that is moth eaten.</p>
