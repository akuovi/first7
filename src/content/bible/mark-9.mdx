## Mark 9

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> And he said unto them, Verily I say unto you, That there be some of them that stand here, which shall not taste of death, till they have seen the kingdom of God come with power.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> And after six days Jesus taketh with him Peter, and James, and John, and leadeth them up into an high mountain apart by themselves: and he was transfigured before them.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> And his raiment became shining, exceeding white as snow; so as no fuller on earth can white them.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> And there appeared unto them Elias with Moses: and they were talking with Jesus.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> And Peter answered and said to Jesus, Master, it is good for us to be here: and let us make three tabernacles; one for thee, and one for Moses, and one for Elias.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> For he wist not what to say; for they were sore afraid.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> And there was a cloud that overshadowed them: and a voice came out of the cloud, saying, This is my beloved Son: hear him.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> And suddenly, when they had looked round about, they saw no man any more, save Jesus only with themselves.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> And as they came down from the mountain, he charged them that they should tell no man what things they had seen, till the Son of man were risen from the dead.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> And they kept that saying with themselves, questioning one with another what the rising from the dead should mean.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> And they asked him, saying, Why say the scribes that Elias must first come?</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> And he answered and told them, Elias verily cometh first, and restoreth all things; and how it is written of the Son of man, that he must suffer many things, and be set at nought.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> But I say unto you, That Elias is indeed come, and they have done unto him whatsoever they listed, as it is written of him.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> And when he came to his disciples, he saw a great multitude about them, and the scribes questioning with them.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> And straightway all the people, when they beheld him, were greatly amazed, and running to him saluted him.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> And he asked the scribes, What question ye with them?</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> And one of the multitude answered and said, Master, I have brought unto thee my son, which hath a dumb spirit;</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> And wheresoever he taketh him, he teareth him: and he foameth, and gnasheth with his teeth, and pineth away: and I spake to thy disciples that they should cast him out; and they could not.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> He answereth him, and saith, O faithless generation, how long shall I be with you? how long shall I suffer you? bring him unto me.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> And they brought him unto him: and when he saw him, straightway the spirit tare him; and he fell on the ground, and wallowed foaming.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> And he asked his father, How long is it ago since this came unto him? And he said, Of a child.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> And ofttimes it hath cast him into the fire, and into the waters, to destroy him: but if thou canst do any thing, have compassion on us, and help us.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> Jesus said unto him, If thou canst believe, all things are possible to him that believeth.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> And straightway the father of the child cried out, and said with tears, Lord, I believe; help thou mine unbelief.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> When Jesus saw that the people came running together, he rebuked the foul spirit, saying unto him, Thou dumb and deaf spirit, I charge thee, come out of him, and enter no more into him.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> And the spirit cried, and rent him sore, and came out of him: and he was as one dead; insomuch that many said, He is dead.</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> But Jesus took him by the hand, and lifted him up; and he arose.</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> And when he was come into the house, his disciples asked him privately, Why could not we cast him out?</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> And he said unto them, This kind can come forth by nothing, but by prayer and fasting.</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> And they departed thence, and passed through Galilee; and he would not that any man should know it.</p>

<p id="v31" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">31</span> For he taught his disciples, and said unto them, The Son of man is delivered into the hands of men, and they shall kill him; and after that he is killed, he shall rise the third day.</p>

<p id="v32" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">32</span> But they understood not that saying, and were afraid to ask him.</p>

<p id="v33" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">33</span> And he came to Capernaum: and being in the house he asked them, What was it that ye disputed among yourselves by the way?</p>

<p id="v34" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">34</span> But they held their peace: for by the way they had disputed among themselves, who should be the greatest.</p>

<p id="v35" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">35</span> And he sat down, and called the twelve, and saith unto them, If any man desire to be first, the same shall be last of all, and servant of all.</p>

<p id="v36" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">36</span> And he took a child, and set him in the midst of them: and when he had taken him in his arms, he said unto them,</p>

<p id="v37" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">37</span> Whosoever shall receive one of such children in my name, receiveth me: and whosoever shall receive me, receiveth not me, but him that sent me.</p>

<p id="v38" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">38</span> And John answered him, saying, Master, we saw one casting out devils in thy name, and he followeth not us: and we forbad him, because he followeth not us.</p>

<p id="v39" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">39</span> But Jesus said, Forbid him not: for there is no man which shall do a miracle in my name, that can lightly speak evil of me.</p>

<p id="v40" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">40</span> For he that is not against us is on our part.</p>

<p id="v41" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">41</span> For whosoever shall give you a cup of water to drink in my name, because ye belong to Christ, verily I say unto you, he shall not lose his reward.</p>

<p id="v42" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">42</span> And whosoever shall offend one of these little ones that believe in me, it is better for him that a millstone were hanged about his neck, and he were cast into the sea.</p>

<p id="v43" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">43</span> And if thy hand offend thee, cut it off: it is better for thee to enter into life maimed, than having two hands to go into hell, into the fire that never shall be quenched:</p>

<p id="v44" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">44</span> Where their worm dieth not, and the fire is not quenched.</p>

<p id="v45" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">45</span> And if thy foot offend thee, cut it off: it is better for thee to enter halt into life, than having two feet to be cast into hell, into the fire that never shall be quenched:</p>

<p id="v46" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">46</span> Where their worm dieth not, and the fire is not quenched.</p>

<p id="v47" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">47</span> And if thine eye offend thee, pluck it out: it is better for thee to enter into the kingdom of God with one eye, than having two eyes to be cast into hell fire:</p>

<p id="v48" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">48</span> Where their worm dieth not, and the fire is not quenched.</p>

<p id="v49" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">49</span> For every one shall be salted with fire, and every sacrifice shall be salted with salt.</p>

<p id="v50" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">50</span> Salt is good: but if the salt have lost his saltness, wherewith will ye season it? Have salt in yourselves, and have peace one with another.</p>
