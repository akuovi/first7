## James 2

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> My brethren, have not the faith of our Lord Jesus Christ, the Lord of glory, with respect of persons.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> For if there come unto your assembly a man with a gold ring, in goodly apparel, and there come in also a poor man in vile raiment;</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> And ye have respect to him that weareth the gay clothing, and say unto him, Sit thou here in a good place; and say to the poor, Stand thou there, or sit here under my footstool:</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Are ye not then partial in yourselves, and are become judges of evil thoughts?</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Hearken, my beloved brethren, Hath not God chosen the poor of this world rich in faith, and heirs of the kingdom which he hath promised to them that love him?</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> But ye have despised the poor. Do not rich men oppress you, and draw you before the judgment seats?</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> Do not they blaspheme that worthy name by the which ye are called?</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> If ye fulfil the royal law according to the scripture, Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself, ye do well:</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> But if ye have respect to persons, ye commit sin, and are convinced of the law as transgressors.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> For whosoever shall keep the whole law, and yet offend in one point, he is guilty of all.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> For he that said, Do not commit adultery, said also, Do not kill. Now if thou commit no adultery, yet if thou kill, thou art become a transgressor of the law.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> So speak ye, and so do, as they that shall be judged by the law of liberty.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> For he shall have judgment without mercy, that hath shewed no mercy; and mercy rejoiceth against judgment.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> What doth it profit, my brethren, though a man say he hath faith, and have not works? can faith save him?</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> If a brother or sister be naked, and destitute of daily food,</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> And one of you say unto them, Depart in peace, be ye warmed and filled; notwithstanding ye give them not those things which are needful to the body; what doth it profit?</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> Even so faith, if it hath not works, is dead, being alone.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> Yea, a man may say, Thou hast faith, and I have works: shew me thy faith without thy works, and I will shew thee my faith by my works.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> Thou believest that there is one God; thou doest well: the devils also believe, and tremble.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> But wilt thou know, O vain man, that faith without works is dead?</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> Was not Abraham our father justified by works, when he had offered Isaac his son upon the altar?</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> Seest thou how faith wrought with his works, and by works was faith made perfect?</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> And the scripture was fulfilled which saith, Abraham believed God, and it was imputed unto him for righteousness: and he was called the Friend of God.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> Ye see then how that by works a man is justified, and not by faith only.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> Likewise also was not Rahab the harlot justified by works, when she had received the messengers, and had sent them out another way?</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> For as the body without the spirit is dead, so faith without works is dead also.</p>
