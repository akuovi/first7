## Isaiah 21

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> The burden of the desert of the sea. As whirlwinds in the south pass through; so it cometh from the desert, from a terrible land.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> A grievous vision is declared unto me; the treacherous dealer dealeth treacherously, and the spoiler spoileth. Go up, O Elam: besiege, O Media; all the sighing thereof have I made to cease.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> Therefore are my loins filled with pain: pangs have taken hold upon me, as the pangs of a woman that travaileth: I was bowed down at the hearing of it; I was dismayed at the seeing of it.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> My heart panted, fearfulness affrighted me: the night of my pleasure hath he turned into fear unto me.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Prepare the table, watch in the watchtower, eat, drink: arise, ye princes, and anoint the shield.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> For thus hath the Lord said unto me, Go, set a watchman, let him declare what he seeth.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> And he saw a chariot with a couple of horsemen, a chariot of asses, and a chariot of camels; and he hearkened diligently with much heed:</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> And he cried, A lion: My lord, I stand continually upon the watchtower in the daytime, and I am set in my ward whole nights:</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> And, behold, here cometh a chariot of men, with a couple of horsemen. And he answered and said, Babylon is fallen, is fallen; and all the graven images of her gods he hath broken unto the ground.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> O my threshing, and the corn of my floor: that which I have heard of the LORD of hosts, the God of Israel, have I declared unto you.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> The burden of Dumah. He calleth to me out of Seir, Watchman, what of the night? Watchman, what of the night?</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> The watchman said, The morning cometh, and also the night: if ye will enquire, enquire ye: return, come.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> The burden upon Arabia. In the forest in Arabia shall ye lodge, O ye travelling companies of Dedanim.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> The inhabitants of the land of Tema brought water to him that was thirsty, they prevented with their bread him that fled.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> For they fled from the swords, from the drawn sword, and from the bent bow, and from the grievousness of war.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> For thus hath the Lord said unto me, Within a year, according to the years of an hireling, and all the glory of Kedar shall fail:</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> And the residue of the number of archers, the mighty men of the children of Kedar, shall be diminished: for the LORD God of Israel hath spoken it.</p>
