## Job 6

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> But Job answered and said,</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> Oh that my grief were throughly weighed, and my calamity laid in the balances together!</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> For now it would be heavier than the sand of the sea: therefore my words are swallowed up.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> For the arrows of the Almighty are within me, the poison whereof drinketh up my spirit: the terrors of God do set themselves in array against me.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Doth the wild ass bray when he hath grass? or loweth the ox over his fodder?</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> Can that which is unsavoury be eaten without salt? or is there any taste in the white of an egg?</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> The things that my soul refused to touch are as my sorrowful meat.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Oh that I might have my request; and that God would grant me the thing that I long for!</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Even that it would please God to destroy me; that he would let loose his hand, and cut me off!</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Then should I yet have comfort; yea, I would harden myself in sorrow: let him not spare; for I have not concealed the words of the Holy One.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> What is my strength, that I should hope? and what is mine end, that I should prolong my life?</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> Is my strength the strength of stones? or is my flesh of brass?</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Is not my help in me? and is wisdom driven quite from me?</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> To him that is afflicted pity should be shewed from his friend; but he forsaketh the fear of the Almighty.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> My brethren have dealt deceitfully as a brook, and as the stream of brooks they pass away;</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> Which are blackish by reason of the ice, and wherein the snow is hid:</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> What time they wax warm, they vanish: when it is hot, they are consumed out of their place.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> The paths of their way are turned aside; they go to nothing, and perish.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> The troops of Tema looked, the companies of Sheba waited for them.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> They were confounded because they had hoped; they came thither, and were ashamed.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> For now ye are nothing; ye see my casting down, and are afraid.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> Did I say, Bring unto me? or, Give a reward for me of your substance?</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> Or, Deliver me from the enemy's hand? or, Redeem me from the hand of the mighty?</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> Teach me, and I will hold my tongue: and cause me to understand wherein I have erred.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> How forcible are right words! but what doth your arguing reprove?</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> Do ye imagine to reprove words, and the speeches of one that is desperate, which are as wind?</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> Yea, ye overwhelm the fatherless, and ye dig a pit for your friend.</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> Now therefore be content, look upon me; for it is evident unto you if I lie.</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> Return, I pray you, let it not be iniquity; yea, return again, my righteousness is in it.</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> Is there iniquity in my tongue? cannot my taste discern perverse things?</p>
