## Ecclesiastes 4

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> So I returned, and considered all the oppressions that are done under the sun: and behold the tears of such as were oppressed, and they had no comforter; and on the side of their oppressors there was power; but they had no comforter.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> Wherefore I praised the dead which are already dead more than the living which are yet alive.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> Yea, better is he than both they, which hath not yet been, who hath not seen the evil work that is done under the sun.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Again, I considered all travail, and every right work, that for this a man is envied of his neighbour. This is also vanity and vexation of spirit.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> The fool foldeth his hands together, and eateth his own flesh.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> Better is an handful with quietness, than both the hands full with travail and vexation of spirit.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> Then I returned, and I saw vanity under the sun.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> There is one alone, and there is not a second; yea, he hath neither child nor brother: yet is there no end of all his labour; neither is his eye satisfied with riches; neither saith he, For whom do I labour, and bereave my soul of good? This is also vanity, yea, it is a sore travail.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Two are better than one; because they have a good reward for their labour.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> For if they fall, the one will lift up his fellow: but woe to him that is alone when he falleth; for he hath not another to help him up.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Again, if two lie together, then they have heat: but how can one be warm alone?</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> And if one prevail against him, two shall withstand him; and a threefold cord is not quickly broken.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Better is a poor and a wise child than an old and foolish king, who will no more be admonished.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> For out of prison he cometh to reign; whereas also he that is born in his kingdom becometh poor.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> I considered all the living which walk under the sun, with the second child that shall stand up in his stead.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> There is no end of all the people, even of all that have been before them: they also that come after shall not rejoice in him. Surely this also is vanity and vexation of spirit.</p>
