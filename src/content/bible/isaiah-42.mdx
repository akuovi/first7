## Isaiah 42

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Behold my servant, whom I uphold; mine elect, in whom my soul delighteth; I have put my spirit upon him: he shall bring forth judgment to the Gentiles.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> He shall not cry, nor lift up, nor cause his voice to be heard in the street.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> A bruised reed shall he not break, and the smoking flax shall he not quench: he shall bring forth judgment unto truth.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> He shall not fail nor be discouraged, till he have set judgment in the earth: and the isles shall wait for his law.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Thus saith God the LORD, he that created the heavens, and stretched them out; he that spread forth the earth, and that which cometh out of it; he that giveth breath unto the people upon it, and spirit to them that walk therein:</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> I the LORD have called thee in righteousness, and will hold thine hand, and will keep thee, and give thee for a covenant of the people, for a light of the Gentiles;</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> To open the blind eyes, to bring out the prisoners from the prison, and them that sit in darkness out of the prison house.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> I am the LORD: that is my name: and my glory will I not give to another, neither my praise to graven images.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Behold, the former things are come to pass, and new things do I declare: before they spring forth I tell you of them.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Sing unto the LORD a new song, and his praise from the end of the earth, ye that go down to the sea, and all that is therein; the isles, and the inhabitants thereof.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> Let the wilderness and the cities thereof lift up their voice, the villages that Kedar doth inhabit: let the inhabitants of the rock sing, let them shout from the top of the mountains.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> Let them give glory unto the LORD, and declare his praise in the islands.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> The LORD shall go forth as a mighty man, he shall stir up jealousy like a man of war: he shall cry, yea, roar; he shall prevail against his enemies.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> I have long time holden my peace; I have been still, and refrained myself: now will I cry like a travailing woman; I will destroy and devour at once.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> I will make waste mountains and hills, and dry up all their herbs; and I will make the rivers islands, and I will dry up the pools.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> And I will bring the blind by a way that they knew not; I will lead them in paths that they have not known: I will make darkness light before them, and crooked things straight. These things will I do unto them, and not forsake them.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> They shall be turned back, they shall be greatly ashamed, that trust in graven images, that say to the molten images, Ye are our gods.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> Hear, ye deaf; and look, ye blind, that ye may see.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> Who is blind, but my servant? or deaf, as my messenger that I sent? who is blind as he that is perfect, and blind as the LORD'S servant?</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> Seeing many things, but thou observest not; opening the ears, but he heareth not.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> The LORD is well pleased for his righteousness' sake; he will magnify the law, and make it honourable.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> But this is a people robbed and spoiled; they are all of them snared in holes, and they are hid in prison houses: they are for a prey, and none delivereth; for a spoil, and none saith, Restore.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> Who among you will give ear to this? who will hearken and hear for the time to come?</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> Who gave Jacob for a spoil, and Israel to the robbers? did not the LORD, he against whom we have sinned? for they would not walk in his ways, neither were they obedient unto his law.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> Therefore he hath poured upon him the fury of his anger, and the strength of battle: and it hath set him on fire round about, yet he knew not; and it burned him, yet he laid it not to heart.</p>
