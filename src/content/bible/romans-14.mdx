## Romans 14

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> Him that is weak in the faith receive ye, but not to doubtful disputations.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> For one believeth that he may eat all things: another, who is weak, eateth herbs.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> Let not him that eateth despise him that eateth not; and let not him which eateth not judge him that eateth: for God hath received him.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Who art thou that judgest another man's servant? to his own master he standeth or falleth. Yea, he shall be holden up: for God is able to make him stand.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> One man esteemeth one day above another: another esteemeth every day alike. Let every man be fully persuaded in his own mind.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> He that regardeth the day, regardeth it unto the Lord; and he that regardeth not the day, to the Lord he doth not regard it. He that eateth, eateth to the Lord, for he giveth God thanks; and he that eateth not, to the Lord he eateth not, and giveth God thanks.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> For none of us liveth to himself, and no man dieth to himself.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> For whether we live, we live unto the Lord; and whether we die, we die unto the Lord: whether we live therefore, or die, we are the Lord's.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> For to this end Christ both died, and rose, and revived, that he might be Lord both of the dead and living.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> But why dost thou judge thy brother? or why dost thou set at nought thy brother? for we shall all stand before the judgment seat of Christ.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> For it is written, As I live, saith the Lord, every knee shall bow to me, and every tongue shall confess to God.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> So then every one of us shall give account of himself to God.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Let us not therefore judge one another any more: but judge this rather, that no man put a stumblingblock or an occasion to fall in his brother's way.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> I know, and am persuaded by the Lord Jesus, that there is nothing unclean of itself: but to him that esteemeth any thing to be unclean, to him it is unclean.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> But if thy brother be grieved with thy meat, now walkest thou not charitably. Destroy not him with thy meat, for whom Christ died.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> Let not then your good be evil spoken of:</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> For the kingdom of God is not meat and drink; but righteousness, and peace, and joy in the Holy Ghost.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> For he that in these things serveth Christ is acceptable to God, and approved of men.</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> Let us therefore follow after the things which make for peace, and things wherewith one may edify another.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> For meat destroy not the work of God. All things indeed are pure; but it is evil for that man who eateth with offence.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> It is good neither to eat flesh, nor to drink wine, nor any thing whereby thy brother stumbleth, or is offended, or is made weak.</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> Hast thou faith? have it to thyself before God. Happy is he that condemneth not himself in that thing which he alloweth.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> And he that doubteth is damned if he eat, because he eateth not of faith: for whatsoever is not of faith is sin.</p>
