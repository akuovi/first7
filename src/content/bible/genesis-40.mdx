## Genesis 40

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> And it came to pass after these things, that the butler of the king of Egypt and his baker had offended their lord the king of Egypt.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> And Pharaoh was wroth against two of his officers, against the chief of the butlers, and against the chief of the bakers.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> And he put them in ward in the house of the captain of the guard, into the prison, the place where Joseph was bound.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> And the captain of the guard charged Joseph with them, and he served them: and they continued a season in ward.</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> And they dreamed a dream both of them, each man his dream in one night, each man according to the interpretation of his dream, the butler and the baker of the king of Egypt, which were bound in the prison.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> And Joseph came in unto them in the morning, and looked upon them, and, behold, they were sad.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> And he asked Pharaoh's officers that were with him in the ward of his lord's house, saying, Wherefore look ye so sadly to day?</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> And they said unto him, We have dreamed a dream, and there is no interpreter of it. And Joseph said unto them, Do not interpretations belong to God? tell me them, I pray you.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> And the chief butler told his dream to Joseph, and said to him, In my dream, behold, a vine was before me;</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> And in the vine were three branches: and it was as though it budded, and her blossoms shot forth; and the clusters thereof brought forth ripe grapes:</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> And Pharaoh's cup was in my hand: and I took the grapes, and pressed them into Pharaoh's cup, and I gave the cup into Pharaoh's hand.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> And Joseph said unto him, This is the interpretation of it: The three branches are three days:</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> Yet within three days shall Pharaoh lift up thine head, and restore thee unto thy place: and thou shalt deliver Pharaoh's cup into his hand, after the former manner when thou wast his butler.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> But think on me when it shall be well with thee, and shew kindness, I pray thee, unto me, and make mention of me unto Pharaoh, and bring me out of this house:</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> For indeed I was stolen away out of the land of the Hebrews: and here also have I done nothing that they should put me into the dungeon.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> When the chief baker saw that the interpretation was good, he said unto Joseph, I also was in my dream, and, behold, I had three white baskets on my head:</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> And in the uppermost basket there was of all manner of bakemeats for Pharaoh; and the birds did eat them out of the basket upon my head.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> And Joseph answered and said, This is the interpretation thereof: The three baskets are three days:</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> Yet within three days shall Pharaoh lift up thy head from off thee, and shall hang thee on a tree; and the birds shall eat thy flesh from off thee.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> And it came to pass the third day, which was Pharaoh's birthday, that he made a feast unto all his servants: and he lifted up the head of the chief butler and of the chief baker among his servants.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> And he restored the chief butler unto his butlership again; and he gave the cup into Pharaoh's hand:</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> But he hanged the chief baker: as Joseph had interpreted to them.</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> Yet did not the chief butler remember Joseph, but forgat him.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> And it came to pass at the end of two full years, that Pharaoh dreamed: and, behold, he stood by the river.</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> And, behold, there came up out of the river seven well favoured kine and fatfleshed; and they fed in a meadow.</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> And, behold, seven other kine came up after them out of the river, ill favoured and leanfleshed; and stood by the other kine upon the brink of the river.</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> And the ill favoured and leanfleshed kine did eat up the seven well favoured and fat kine. So Pharaoh awoke.</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> And he slept and dreamed the second time: and, behold, seven ears of corn came up upon one stalk, rank and good.</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> And, behold, seven thin ears and blasted with the east wind sprung up after them.</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> And the seven thin ears devoured the seven rank and full ears. And Pharaoh awoke, and, behold, it was a dream.</p>

<p id="v31" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">31</span> And it came to pass in the morning that his spirit was troubled; and he sent and called for all the magicians of Egypt, and all the wise men thereof: and Pharaoh told them his dream; but there was none that could interpret them unto Pharaoh.</p>

<p id="v32" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">32</span> Then spake the chief butler unto Pharaoh, saying, I do remember my faults this day:</p>

<p id="v33" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">33</span> Pharaoh was wroth with his servants, and put me in ward in the captain of the guard's house, both me and the chief baker:</p>

<p id="v34" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">34</span> And we dreamed a dream in one night, I and he; we dreamed each man according to the interpretation of his dream.</p>

<p id="v35" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">35</span> And there was there with us a young man, an Hebrew, servant to the captain of the guard; and we told him, and he interpreted to us our dreams; to each man according to his dream he did interpret.</p>

<p id="v36" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">36</span> And it came to pass, as he interpreted to us, so it was; me he restored unto mine office, and him he hanged.</p>

<p id="v37" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">37</span> Then Pharaoh sent and called Joseph, and they brought him hastily out of the dungeon: and he shaved himself, and changed his raiment, and came in unto Pharaoh.</p>

<p id="v38" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">38</span> And Pharaoh said unto Joseph, I have dreamed a dream, and there is none that can interpret it: and I have heard say of thee, that thou canst understand a dream to interpret it.</p>

<p id="v39" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">39</span> And Joseph answered Pharaoh, saying, It is not in me: God shall give Pharaoh an answer of peace.</p>

<p id="v40" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">40</span> And Pharaoh said unto Joseph, In my dream, behold, I stood upon the bank of the river:</p>

<p id="v41" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">41</span> And, behold, there came up out of the river seven kine, fatfleshed and well favoured; and they fed in a meadow:</p>

<p id="v42" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">42</span> And, behold, seven other kine came up after them, poor and very ill favoured and leanfleshed, such as I never saw in all the land of Egypt for badness:</p>

<p id="v43" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">43</span> And the lean and the ill favoured kine did eat up the first seven fat kine:</p>

<p id="v44" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">44</span> And when they had eaten them up, it could not be known that they had eaten them; but they were still ill favoured, as at the beginning. So I awoke.</p>

<p id="v45" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">45</span> And I saw in my dream, and, behold, seven ears came up in one stalk, full and good:</p>

<p id="v46" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">46</span> And, behold, seven ears, withered, thin, and blasted with the east wind, sprung up after them:</p>

<p id="v47" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">47</span> And the thin ears devoured the seven good ears: and I told this unto the magicians; but there was none that could declare it to me.</p>

<p id="v48" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">48</span> And Joseph said unto Pharaoh, The dream of Pharaoh is one: God hath shewed Pharaoh what he is about to do.</p>

<p id="v49" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">49</span> The seven good kine are seven years; and the seven good ears are seven years: the dream is one.</p>

<p id="v50" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">50</span> And the seven thin and ill favoured kine that came up after them are seven years; and the seven empty ears blasted with the east wind shall be seven years of famine.</p>

<p id="v51" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">51</span> This is the thing which I have spoken unto Pharaoh: What God is about to do he sheweth unto Pharaoh.</p>

<p id="v52" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">52</span> Behold, there come seven years of great plenty throughout all the land of Egypt:</p>

<p id="v53" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">53</span> And there shall arise after them seven years of famine; and all the plenty shall be forgotten in the land of Egypt; and the famine shall consume the land;</p>

<p id="v54" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">54</span> And the plenty shall not be known in the land by reason of that famine following; for it shall be very grievous.</p>

<p id="v55" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">55</span> And for that the dream was doubled unto Pharaoh twice; it is because the thing is established by God, and God will shortly bring it to pass.</p>

<p id="v56" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">56</span> Now therefore let Pharaoh look out a man discreet and wise, and set him over the land of Egypt.</p>

<p id="v57" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">57</span> Let Pharaoh do this, and let him appoint officers over the land, and take up the fifth part of the land of Egypt in the seven plenteous years.</p>

<p id="v58" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">58</span> And let them gather all the food of those good years that come, and lay up corn under the hand of Pharaoh, and let them keep food in the cities.</p>

<p id="v59" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">59</span> And that food shall be for store to the land against the seven years of famine, which shall be in the land of Egypt; that the land perish not through the famine.</p>

<p id="v60" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">60</span> And the thing was good in the eyes of Pharaoh, and in the eyes of all his servants.</p>

<p id="v61" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">61</span> And Pharaoh said unto his servants, Can we find such a one as this is, a man in whom the Spirit of God is?</p>

<p id="v62" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">62</span> And Pharaoh said unto Joseph, Forasmuch as God hath shewed thee all this, there is none so discreet and wise as thou art:</p>

<p id="v63" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">63</span> Thou shalt be over my house, and according unto thy word shall all my people be ruled: only in the throne will I be greater than thou.</p>

<p id="v64" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">64</span> And Pharaoh said unto Joseph, See, I have set thee over all the land of Egypt.</p>

<p id="v65" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">65</span> And Pharaoh took off his ring from his hand, and put it upon Joseph's hand, and arrayed him in vestures of fine linen, and put a gold chain about his neck;</p>

<p id="v66" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">66</span> And he made him to ride in the second chariot which he had; and they cried before him, Bow the knee: and he made him ruler over all the land of Egypt.</p>

<p id="v67" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">67</span> And Pharaoh said unto Joseph, I am Pharaoh, and without thee shall no man lift up his hand or foot in all the land of Egypt.</p>

<p id="v68" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">68</span> And Pharaoh called Joseph's name Zaphnath-paaneah; and he gave him to wife Asenath the daughter of Poti-pherah priest of On. And Joseph went out over all the land of Egypt.</p>

<p id="v69" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">69</span> And Joseph was thirty years old when he stood before Pharaoh king of Egypt. And Joseph went out from the presence of Pharaoh, and went throughout all the land of Egypt.</p>

<p id="v70" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">70</span> And in the seven plenteous years the earth brought forth by handfuls.</p>

<p id="v71" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">71</span> And he gathered up all the food of the seven years, which were in the land of Egypt, and laid up the food in the cities: the food of the field, which was round about every city, laid he up in the same.</p>

<p id="v72" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">72</span> And Joseph gathered corn as the sand of the sea, very much, until he left numbering; for it was without number.</p>

<p id="v73" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">73</span> And unto Joseph were born two sons before the years of famine came, which Asenath the daughter of Poti-pherah priest of On bare unto him.</p>

<p id="v74" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">74</span> And Joseph called the name of the firstborn Manasseh: For God, said he, hath made me forget all my toil, and all my father's house.</p>

<p id="v75" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">75</span> And the name of the second called he Ephraim: For God hath caused me to be fruitful in the land of my affliction.</p>

<p id="v76" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">76</span> And the seven years of plenteousness, that was in the land of Egypt, were ended.</p>

<p id="v77" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">77</span> And the seven years of dearth began to come, according as Joseph had said: and the dearth was in all lands; but in all the land of Egypt there was bread.</p>

<p id="v78" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">78</span> And when all the land of Egypt was famished, the people cried to Pharaoh for bread: and Pharaoh said unto all the Egyptians, Go unto Joseph; what he saith to you, do.</p>

<p id="v79" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">79</span> And the famine was over all the face of the earth: And Joseph opened all the storehouses, and sold unto the Egyptians; and the famine waxed sore in the land of Egypt.</p>

<p id="v80" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">80</span> And all countries came into Egypt to Joseph for to buy corn; because that the famine was so sore in all lands.</p>
