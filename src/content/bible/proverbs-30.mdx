## Proverbs 30

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> The words of Agur the son of Jakeh, even the prophecy: the man spake unto Ithiel, even unto Ithiel and Ucal,</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> Surely I am more brutish than any man, and have not the understanding of a man.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> I neither learned wisdom, nor have the knowledge of the holy.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> Who hath ascended up into heaven, or descended? who hath gathered the wind in his fists? who hath bound the waters in a garment? who hath established all the ends of the earth? what is his name, and what is his son's name, if thou canst tell?</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Every word of God is pure: he is a shield unto them that put their trust in him.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> Add thou not unto his words, lest he reprove thee, and thou be found a liar.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> Two things have I required of thee; deny me them not before I die:</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Remove far from me vanity and lies: give me neither poverty nor riches; feed me with food convenient for me:</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Lest I be full, and deny thee, and say, Who is the LORD? or lest I be poor, and steal, and take the name of my God in vain.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> Accuse not a servant unto his master, lest he curse thee, and thou be found guilty.</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> There is a generation that curseth their father, and doth not bless their mother.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> There is a generation that are pure in their own eyes, and yet is not washed from their filthiness.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> There is a generation, O how lofty are their eyes! and their eyelids are lifted up.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> There is a generation, whose teeth are as swords, and their jaw teeth as knives, to devour the poor from off the earth, and the needy from among men.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> The horseleach hath two daughters, crying, Give, give. There are three things that are never satisfied, yea, four things say not, It is enough:</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> The grave; and the barren womb; the earth that is not filled with water; and the fire that saith not, It is enough.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> The eye that mocketh at his father, and despiseth to obey his mother, the ravens of the valley shall pick it out, and the young eagles shall eat it.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> There be three things which are too wonderful for me, yea, four which I know not:</p>

<p id="v19" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">19</span> The way of an eagle in the air; the way of a serpent upon a rock; the way of a ship in the midst of the sea; and the way of a man with a maid.</p>

<p id="v20" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">20</span> Such is the way of an adulterous woman; she eateth, and wipeth her mouth, and saith, I have done no wickedness.</p>

<p id="v21" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">21</span> For three things the earth is disquieted, and for four which it cannot bear:</p>

<p id="v22" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">22</span> For a servant when he reigneth; and a fool when he is filled with meat;</p>

<p id="v23" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">23</span> For an odious woman when she is married; and an handmaid that is heir to her mistress.</p>

<p id="v24" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">24</span> There be four things which are little upon the earth, but they are exceeding wise:</p>

<p id="v25" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">25</span> The ants are a people not strong, yet they prepare their meat in the summer;</p>

<p id="v26" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">26</span> The conies are but a feeble folk, yet make they their houses in the rocks;</p>

<p id="v27" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">27</span> The locusts have no king, yet go they forth all of them by bands;</p>

<p id="v28" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">28</span> The spider taketh hold with her hands, and is in kings' palaces.</p>

<p id="v29" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">29</span> There be three things which go well, yea, four are comely in going:</p>

<p id="v30" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">30</span> A lion which is strongest among beasts, and turneth not away for any;</p>

<p id="v31" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">31</span> A greyhound; an he goat also; and a king, against whom there is no rising up.</p>

<p id="v32" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">32</span> If thou hast done foolishly in lifting up thyself, or if thou hast thought evil, lay thine hand upon thy mouth.</p>

<p id="v33" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">33</span> Surely the churning of milk bringeth forth butter, and the wringing of the nose bringeth forth blood: so the forcing of wrath bringeth forth strife.</p>
