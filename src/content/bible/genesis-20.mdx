## Genesis 20

<p id="v1" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">1</span> And Abraham journeyed from thence toward the south country, and dwelled between Kadesh and Shur, and sojourned in Gerar.</p>

<p id="v2" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">2</span> And Abraham said of Sarah his wife, She is my sister: and Abimelech king of Gerar sent, and took Sarah.</p>

<p id="v3" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">3</span> But God came to Abimelech in a dream by night, and said to him, Behold, thou art but a dead man, for the woman which thou hast taken; for she is a man's wife.</p>

<p id="v4" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">4</span> But Abimelech had not come near her: and he said, Lord, wilt thou slay also a righteous nation?</p>

<p id="v5" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">5</span> Said he not unto me, She is my sister? and she, even she herself said, He is my brother: in the integrity of my heart and innocency of my hands have I done this.</p>

<p id="v6" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">6</span> And God said unto him in a dream, Yea, I know that thou didst this in the integrity of thy heart; for I also withheld thee from sinning against me: therefore suffered I thee not to touch her.</p>

<p id="v7" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">7</span> Now therefore restore the man his wife; for he is a prophet, and he shall pray for thee, and thou shalt live: and if thou restore her not, know thou that thou shalt surely die, thou, and all that are thine.</p>

<p id="v8" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">8</span> Therefore Abimelech rose early in the morning, and called all his servants, and told all these things in their ears: and the men were sore afraid.</p>

<p id="v9" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">9</span> Then Abimelech called Abraham, and said unto him, What hast thou done unto us? and what have I offended thee, that thou hast brought on me and on my kingdom a great sin? thou hast done deeds unto me that ought not to be done.</p>

<p id="v10" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">10</span> And Abimelech said unto Abraham, What sawest thou, that thou hast done this thing?</p>

<p id="v11" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">11</span> And Abraham said, Because I thought, Surely the fear of God is not in this place; and they will slay me for my wife's sake.</p>

<p id="v12" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">12</span> And yet indeed she is my sister; she is the daughter of my father, but not the daughter of my mother; and she became my wife.</p>

<p id="v13" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">13</span> And it came to pass, when God caused me to wander from my father's house, that I said unto her, This is thy kindness which thou shalt shew unto me; at every place whither we shall come, say of me, He is my brother.</p>

<p id="v14" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">14</span> And Abimelech took sheep, and oxen, and menservants, and womenservants, and gave them unto Abraham, and restored him Sarah his wife.</p>

<p id="v15" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">15</span> And Abimelech said, Behold, my land is before thee: dwell where it pleaseth thee.</p>

<p id="v16" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">16</span> And unto Sarah he said, Behold, I have given thy brother a thousand pieces of silver: behold, he is to thee a covering of the eyes, unto all that are with thee, and with all other: thus she was reproved.</p>

<p id="v17" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">17</span> So Abraham prayed unto God: and God healed Abimelech, and his wife, and his maidservants; and they bare children.</p>

<p id="v18" class="bible-verse"><span class="verse-number">18</span> For the LORD had fast closed up all the wombs of the house of Abimelech, because of Sarah Abraham's wife.</p>
